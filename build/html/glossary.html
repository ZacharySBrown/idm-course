DSP primitives — 31 terms

additive synthesisadditive_synthesis
Building complex sounds by summing sinusoids at independently controlled frequencies, amplitudes, and phases. The Fourier-theorem approach to synthesis: in principle any periodic timbre can be reconstructed from enough sines. In practice expensive, which is why most synths cheat with subtractive or FM instead.
wikipedia
see also: subtractive_synthesisfftwavetable_synthesisbib:roads_computer_music_tutorial
aliasingaliasing
What happens when a signal contains frequencies above the Nyquist frequency: they fold back down into the audible range as spurious tones. On an Akai S950 this is part of the aesthetic — Squarepusher explicitly treats crude S950 repitching as a feature of drum & bass. On a mastering limiter it is a defect.
wikipedia lesson: w07-squarepusher-chops
see also: nyquist_frequencysample_rateanti_aliasing_filtervarispeedbib:tape_op_89bib:roads_computer_music_tutorialsong:squarepusher_come_on
AM synthesisam_synthesis
Amplitude-modulation synthesis — one signal scales the amplitude of another at audio rate. Produces two sidebands (sum and difference frequencies) around the carrier. Simpler spectrum than FM; closely related to ring modulation, which is AM without the DC component.
wikipedia
see also: ring_modulationfm_synthesissidebandbib:roads_computer_music_tutorial
bit depthbit_depth
Number of bits per sample — sets the dynamic range. 16-bit = 96 dB, 24-bit = 144 dB, 32-bit float = effectively unlimited for intermediate processing. The SP-1200's 12-bit/26 kHz constraint is what makes every hip-hop record from 1987–1993 sound like it does.
wikipedia
see also: sample_ratefloat_32bitcrush
carriercarrier
In FM/AM synthesis, the oscillator being modulated — the one whose output you hear. Contrast with modulator, the one doing the modulating. On a DX7 algorithm diagram, carriers sit at the bottom of the stack.
wikipedia
see also: modulatorfm_synthesisoperator
convolutionconvolution
A mathematical operation that applies one signal (an impulse response) as a filter to another. Convolution reverbs use a room's recorded impulse to re-imprint that room's acoustics on arbitrary audio. Also the basis of FFT-based filtering.
wikipedia
see also: fftfilter
FFTfft
Fast Fourier Transform — an O(N log N) algorithm that decomposes a signal into its frequency components. The underlying math of every spectrogram, EQ analyzer, and phase vocoder in your session.
wikipedia
see also: ifftphase_vocoderconvolutionadditive_synthesisbib:roads_computer_music_tutorial
filterfilter
A circuit or algorithm that lets some frequencies through and attenuates others. Four basic types: low-pass (LP), high-pass (HP), band-pass (BP), notch (band-reject). The dominant sound-design tool of subtractive synthesis and of every mixer's EQ.
wikipedia
see also: filter_sloperesonanceq_factoreqsubtractive_synthesis
filter slopefilter_slope
How steeply a filter attenuates past its cutoff, measured in dB per octave. 6 dB/oct is gentle (one-pole). 12 dB/oct is the classic Korg MS-20 and Oberheim sound. 24 dB/oct is the Moog ladder. Steeper is not better — it is more surgical and less musical.
wikipedia
see also: filterresonancebib:korg_ms20_manual
FM synthesisfm_synthesis
Frequency-modulation synthesis — one oscillator (the modulator) varies the frequency of another (the carrier) at audio rate, producing sidebands that extend far beyond the original two pitches. Commercialized by Yamaha on the DX7 in 1983 under license from John Chowning's 1967 Stanford work. Cheap to compute, rich in inharmonic content, notoriously hard to dial in by ear.
wikipedia ccrma.stanford.edu lesson: w08-autechre-feedback
see also: operatorsidebandcarriermodulatorphase_modulationadditive_synthesisbib:roads_computer_music_tutorial
formantformant
A concentration of acoustic energy at a particular frequency — the resonances of the vocal tract that make 'ah' sound different from 'ee' at the same pitch. When you pitch-shift a voice without preserving formants, you get chipmunks. When you preserve them, you get plausible transposition.
wikipedia lesson: w09-vocal-chopping
see also: pitch_shiftinglittle_alterboycomplex_pro_warp
granular synthesisgranular_synthesis
Sound built from thousands of short grains (1–100 ms slices) of a source, scattered in time and pitch. Theorized by Iannis Xenakis, formalized by Curtis Roads. In practice: feed any field recording to Granulator III and turn the Scan knob.
wikipedia roberthenke.com
see also: samplerphase_vocoderpaulstretchbib:roads_computer_music_tutorialbib:henke_granulator_iii
IFFTifft
Inverse FFT — converts frequency-domain data back to the time domain. Always paired with an FFT on the way out of a spectral process. In image-to-sound tools like MetaSynth or Photosounder, the IFFT is where the drawing becomes audio.
wikipedia uisoftware.com
see also: fftphase_vocoderbib:metasynth_uisoftwarebib:photosounder_docs
linear interpolationlinear_interpolation
Reconstructing sample values between known samples by drawing a straight line. Cheap, fast, audibly compromised at high pitch-shift ratios — you hear it as the grain in old samplers. The SP-1200 and S950 sound is partly this.
wikipedia
see also: sinc_interpolationsampleraliasing
modulatormodulator
In FM/AM synthesis, the oscillator that modulates another. Its frequency relative to the carrier (the ratio) determines harmonicity; its amplitude determines how many sidebands get produced. Hide a modulator behind a carrier to get bell tones; expose it to get noise.
wikipedia
see also: carrierfm_synthesisoperator
noise (white/pink/brownian)noise_colors
White noise is equal power per hertz (flat spectrum). Pink noise is equal power per octave — the canonical test signal for room calibration because it matches human frequency weighting. Brownian (red) noise drops another 3 dB/oct lower still, rumbly and deep. All three are standard sources in Meld and Roar.
wikipedia
see also: subtractive_synthesis
Nyquist frequencynyquist_frequency
Half the sample rate — the highest frequency a digital system can represent without aliasing. At 44.1 kHz, that's 22.05 kHz. Push content above it and the system folds it back down as inharmonic garbage unless you low-pass first.
wikipedia
see also: aliasingsample_rateanti_aliasing_filterbib:roads_computer_music_tutorial
operator (FM)operator
The unit cell of an FM synth: a sine oscillator with its own envelope generator. On the DX7 there are six; they can be routed as carriers, modulators, or feedback paths via one of 32 algorithms. Ableton's Operator instrument keeps the name and the topology.
wikipedia www.ableton.com
see also: fm_synthesiscarriermodulatorsidebandbib:ableton_live_12_manual
phase modulationphase_modulation
Modulating the phase — rather than the frequency — of a carrier oscillator with another signal. Mathematically equivalent to FM in steady state, and it is what every DX-family Yamaha synth actually does under the hood. Chowning called it FM because the patent was easier to write that way.
wikipedia ccrma.stanford.edu
see also: fm_synthesisoperatorcarrierbib:roads_computer_music_tutorial
phase vocoderphase_vocoder
An FFT-based algorithm that decomposes a signal into short overlapping spectral frames, allowing independent manipulation of time and pitch. The engine behind most time-stretch plugins — Ableton's Complex and Complex Pro warp modes, Elastique, PaulStretch.
wikipedia lesson: w04-warp-modes
see also: ffttime_stretchingpitch_shiftingcomplex_pro_warpbib:roads_computer_music_tutorialbib:paulxstretch_docs
physical modelingphysical_modeling
Synthesis by simulating the physics of real instruments — bowed strings, struck membranes, plucked bars. Yamaha VL1 (1993) was the first commercial example. Ableton's Collision and Tension (and the resonator models in Meld — Plate, Membrane) are modern examples.
wikipedia
see also: formantconvolution
Q factorq_factor
Quality factor — the narrowness of a filter's peak or notch. High Q = narrow, surgical. Low Q = broad, musical. On a parametric EQ, Q is the knob that decides whether you're sculpting or drawing.
wikipedia
see also: filterresonanceeq
resonanceresonance
Emphasis of frequencies at a filter's cutoff point. Push resonance high and the filter begins to self-oscillate into a pure sine. This is the squelch of a TB-303 and the scream of an MS-20 HPF.
wikipedia
see also: filterq_factorfilter_slopebib:korg_ms20_manual
ring modulationring_modulation
Multiplication of two audio signals — produces sum and difference sidebands only, no original carriers. The source of all metallic bell and robot-voice timbres; staple of 1970s BBC Radiophonic Workshop and of every sci-fi film sound ever.
wikipedia
see also: am_synthesissidebandfm_synthesis
sample ratesample_rate
Number of samples per second used to represent a continuous signal digitally. CD = 44.1 kHz. Pro audio usually 48/96 kHz. Higher sample rates buy you headroom above the audible band (and fewer aliasing artifacts from nonlinear processing), not 'more detail' in the music.
wikipedia
see also: nyquist_frequencybit_depthaliasing
samplersampler
A digital instrument that plays back recorded audio — usually pitched and triggered by MIDI. Fairlight CMI (1979), E-mu Emulator, Akai S900/S950, MPC line. In Ableton, Simpler and Sampler. The shape of popular music from 1985 onward.
wikipedia lesson: w03-simpler-basics
see also: simpler_modesslice_to_midiresampleone_shotbib:akai_mpc3000_manualbib:ensoniq_asr10_manual
sidebandsideband
A frequency component produced by modulation, sitting at sum and difference of carrier and modulator frequencies. FM produces an infinite series of sidebands whose amplitudes follow Bessel functions. That is why FM sounds metallic even at simple ratios.
wikipedia
see also: fm_synthesisam_synthesiscarriermodulatorbib:roads_computer_music_tutorial
sinc interpolationsinc_interpolation
Theoretically perfect reconstruction of a band-limited signal using the sinc function as a kernel. In practice everyone uses a windowed approximation (Lanczos, Kaiser). This is what a high-quality pitch-shifter uses where SP-1200 uses crude point-resample.
wikipedia
see also: linear_interpolationpitch_shiftingaliasingbib:roads_computer_music_tutorial
subtractive synthesissubtractive_synthesis
Start with a harmonically rich waveform (saw, square, noise), sculpt it with filters. The classic analog-synth approach — Minimoog, MS-20, SH-101. Sound design is mostly filter design.
wikipedia
see also: filterfilter_sloperesonanceadditive_synthesisbib:korg_ms20_manualbib:roads_computer_music_tutorial
synthesizersynthesizer
An electronic instrument that generates sound from first principles — oscillators, filters, envelopes, modulation. As opposed to a sampler, which plays recordings. Moog, Buchla, ARP, Prophet, DX7, Meld.
wikipedia
see also: subtractive_synthesisfm_synthesiswavetable_synthesissampler
wavetable synthesiswavetable_synthesis
Sound generation via an indexed table of single-cycle waveforms, interpolating between them over time. Popularized by the PPG Wave and Waldorf; in Ableton, it is the Wavetable instrument. Sweeping through the table is how you get the morphing timbre that defines a lot of modern bass music.
wikipedia
see also: additive_synthesisgranular_synthesisbib:ableton_live_12_manual

Mix & master — 18 terms

dBFSdbfs
Decibels relative to full scale — the scale on every digital meter. 0 dBFS is the maximum representable sample; everything below is negative. Not the same as SPL (sound pressure level) and not the same as LUFS (perceived loudness).
wikipedia
see also: lufstrue_peakrms
EQeq
Equalization — shaping frequency balance via filters. Standard shapes: shelf (boost/cut everything above or below a corner), bell/peak (boost/cut around a center), notch (very narrow bell), pass (HP/LP with slope). Ableton EQ Eight offers all four.
wikipedia
see also: filterq_factorfilter_slope
equal loudness contourequal_loudness_contour
Curves (Fletcher-Munson 1933, refined as ISO 226) showing which sound pressure levels are perceived as equally loud across frequency. The reason mixes sound bass- and treble-heavy at low volumes — human hearing compresses its range down there. Why you check mixes at multiple listening levels.
wikipedia lesson: w12-ship-two-tracks
see also: lufsheadphone_translation
gatinggating
Muting a signal below a threshold. Opposite of a compressor in directional feel: compressors pull loud things down, gates kill quiet things off. Useful for cleaning drum bleed and for creating hard, stuttery rhythmic FX when keyed from MIDI or another signal.
wikipedia
see also: sidechain_compressiontransient_shaping
Haas effecthaas_effect
Also called the precedence effect or law of the first wavefront. When the same sound reaches you twice within about 2–40 ms, you perceive one event, localized to the first arrival. Delay one side of a stereo source by 15 ms and the mono signal appears to shift toward the undelayed ear — an old mixing trick for cheap width.
wikipedia
see also: phase_cancellationstereo_imagingmid_side
Little AlterBoylittle_alterboy
SoundToys pitch/formant box. Transpose mode with Pitch +7 / Formant +7 linked gives Kanye-chipmunk vocal; unlinked (Formant -8, Pitch 0) turns any source bass-y and textured. Robot mode with MIDI input plus Drive 5 = instant vocoder lead. Used on three-moves vocal processing throughout the course.
www.soundtoys.com lesson: w09-vocal-chopping
see also: formantpitch_shiftingmicroshiftchipmunk_soulbib:soundtoys_little_alterboy_manual
loudness warloudness_war
The multi-decade trend (peaking roughly 1995–2010) of masters getting louder at the expense of dynamic range, driven by the assumption louder = better on radio. Streaming loudness normalization (Spotify, YouTube, Apple) ended the commercial incentive. Most competent mastering now targets LUFS, not peak.
wikipedia lesson: w12-ship-two-tracks
see also: lufsnormalizationtrue_peak
LUFSlufs
Loudness Units relative to Full Scale — the EBU R128 / ITU-R BS.1770 standard for perceived loudness, measured over time with K-weighting. Streaming platforms target roughly -14 LUFS integrated (Spotify, Apple Music); mastering for vinyl or club -8 to -10. Replaced RMS as the honest loudness metric.
wikipedia tech.ebu.ch lesson: w12-ship-two-tracks
see also: true_peakdbfsrmsloudness_warnormalization
MicroShiftmicroshift
SoundToys plugin emulating the Eventide H3000 and AMS DMX 15-80 stereo-widening shifts — tiny pitch detunes with tiny delays. Style I = H3000 preset 231 (classic widener), Style II = H3000 preset 519 (warmer), Style III = AMS DMX (wider, more aggressive). The Kanye/MBDTF-era vocal-stack engine. Focus at 200–400 Hz to keep lows mono.
www.soundtoys.com lesson: w09-vocal-chopping
see also: stereo_imaginghaas_effectpitch_shiftingbib:soundtoys_microshift_manual
mid-sidemid_side
A stereo representation in two channels: mid = L+R, side = L-R. Letting you EQ or compress the center independently of the stereo sides. The trick for gluing a bass to mono while keeping reverb wide.
wikipedia
see also: stereo_imagingeq
multiband compressionmultiband_compression
Splits a signal into frequency bands, compresses each independently, recombines. Useful for taming a boomy low end without squashing a bright top. FabFilter Pro-MB, iZotope Ozone. Overuse it and your mix sounds like glass.
wikipedia
see also: sidechain_compressioneqbib:izotope_ozone_maximizer_docs
normalizationnormalization
Scaling a signal so a chosen metric hits a chosen target. Peak normalization: loudest sample = 0 dBFS (or some ceiling). Loudness normalization: integrated LUFS = target. Streaming services apply the latter at playback; it is why your overcompressed master will not sound louder than a dynamic one.
wikipedia
see also: lufsloudness_wartrue_peak
phase cancellationphase_cancellation
When two identical or near-identical signals sum out of phase, they cancel. Two perfectly inverted signals = silence. Partial cancellation is what makes multi-mic drum recordings and layered kicks sound thin. Align transients within ±2 ms and you're mostly safe.
wikipedia lesson: w06-fourtet-layering
see also: haas_effectstereo_imagingbib:attack_magazine_drum_layering
RMSrms
Root-mean-square level — a measure of average signal power, closer to perceived loudness than peak but crude compared to LUFS. Pro compressors and meters show both peak and RMS; the gap between them is your crest factor.
wikipedia
see also: lufsdbfs
sidechain compressionsidechain_compression
Using one signal to trigger a compressor on another — typically a kick ducking a bass or pad. Ableton's Compressor exposes the external sidechain natively. Squarepusher treats it as compositional ('on The Metallurgist, the bass drum is the sidechain source'), not as a corrective tool.
wikipedia lesson: w07-squarepusher-chops
see also: gatingmultiband_compressionbib:tape_op_89
stereo imagingstereo_imaging
The perceived left/right placement and width of a mix. Controlled by pan, level, Haas delay, mid-side balance, and short early reflections. Mono compatibility still matters because phone speakers and club PAs both collapse the sides.
wikipedia
see also: mid_sidehaas_effectmicroshift
transient shapingtransient_shaping
Level-adjusting the attack portion of a sound independently of its sustain — boost attack to sharpen a snare, reduce it to soften. Works by envelope-following rather than threshold. Ableton's Drum Buss has this baked in.
wikipedia
see also: gatingkronecker_king_snare
true peaktrue_peak
Inter-sample peak — the actual analog peak a digital signal will produce after reconstruction, which can exceed the sample-peak reading by several dB. A limiter that only watches sample peaks will clip on playback. Streaming ceilings are specified at -1 dBTP for this reason.
wikipedia lesson: w12-ship-two-tracks
see also: lufsdbfsnormalizationbib:fabfilter_prol2_docs

Time & rhythm — 13 terms

beat trackingbeat_tracking
Algorithmic estimation of BPM and beat locations in audio. What happens when you drag an untempo'd clip into an Ableton session — the software runs a beat tracker and draws warp markers. Failure modes: half-tempo detection, phase-slip on tracks without strong kick.
wikipedia
see also: warpbpmtempo_mapping
BPMbpm
Beats per minute. At 120 BPM one beat is 500 ms; one 16th is 125 ms. Everyone knows this. The point of writing it down is that every delay time, every Haas offset, every swing in milliseconds only means something relative to BPM.
wikipedia
see also: quantizationswingmicrotiming
Groove Poolgroove_pool
Ableton's repository of groove templates (Cmd/Ctrl+Alt+6). Extract Groove from any audio clip, drop on MIDI clips. Timing (0–130%) controls swing strength, Random adds micro-ms jitter, Base sets the grid. The mechanism for applying Dilla-style per-voice grooves without hand-nudging every note.
wikipedia www.ableton.com
see also: swingmicrotimingdilla_timequantizationbib:ableton_live_12_manual
microtimingmicrotiming
Sub-grid timing deviations — 5, 10, 20 ms ahead of or behind the beat. Dilla drags snares 20–30 ms late. Four Tet applies 15% MIDI randomization to velocity and octave. Microtiming is where groove lives and where quantize kills it.
wikipedia lesson: w09-vocal-chopping
see also: quantizationswingdilla_timebib:charnas_dilla_timebib:tape_notes_140_four_tetsong:dilla_dont_cry
pitch shiftingpitch_shifting
Changing pitch without changing duration (or vice versa). Mechanically tape does the opposite — varispeed links pitch and tempo. Modern pitch shifters decouple them via phase vocoder or granular techniques, with optional formant preservation.
wikipedia
see also: time_stretchingformantvarispeedrepitch_warp
polymeterpolymeter
Two or more parts cycling at different bar lengths over the same pulse — e.g., a 5/16 hi-hat pattern against a 4/4 kick. They drift out of alignment and reconverge after the least common multiple of their lengths. Autechre's bread and butter.
wikipedia lesson: w08-autechre-feedback
see also: polyrhythmclip_envelopesbib:ali_jamieson_polyrhythmsong:autechre_parhelic
polyrhythmpolyrhythm
Two or more rhythmic cycles with different subdivisions played simultaneously, most commonly 3-against-2 or 4-against-3. Distinct from polymeter, which is different bar lengths at a shared pulse.
wikipedia lesson: w08-autechre-feedback
see also: polymetermicrotimingbib:ali_jamieson_polyrhythmbib:polyrhythmus_m4l
quantizationquantization
Snapping MIDI (or audio transients) to a grid. Straight quantization is the robot baseline; partial quantization at 50–80% strength pulls performances toward the grid without killing feel. J Dilla's signature is recorded with quantize disabled entirely.
wikipedia
see also: swingmicrotimingdilla_timegroove_poolbib:charnas_dilla_time
swingswing
Delaying every other 16th (or 8th) to produce a triplet-feel lilt. 50% = straight. 66% = hard triplet swing. The MPC's per-pad swing — different amounts on different voices within a single bar — is the mechanism Dilla exploited.
wikipedia lesson: w09-vocal-chopping
see also: quantizationmicrotimingdilla_timegroove_poolbib:charnas_dilla_timebib:akai_mpc3000_manual
tempo mappingtempo_mapping
Placing tempo changes along a timeline so a DAW's grid follows a performance rather than forcing the performance to the grid. Useful when importing live drum takes or rubato pianos. Ableton 11+ added this via the Arrangement tempo track.
wikipedia
see also: bpmbeat_trackingwarpbib:ableton_live_12_manual
time stretchingtime_stretching
Changing the duration of audio without changing pitch. Usually accomplished via phase vocoder (Complex, Complex Pro), granular resynthesis (Texture mode), or transient-preserving resampling (Beats mode). Every stretch algorithm has an aesthetic; pick one for its artifacts.
wikipedia lesson: w04-warp-modes
see also: pitch_shiftingphase_vocodercomplex_pro_warpbeats_warpwarpbib:ableton_live_12_manual
transient detectiontransient_detection
Automated identification of note onsets in audio via short-time energy analysis. The basis of Ableton's Slice to MIDI, Beats warp mode, and Groove Pool extraction. Bad transient detection on a mushy kick is where the work happens.
wikipedia lesson: w03-simpler-basics
see also: slice_to_midibeats_warpbeat_tracking
warpwarp
Ableton's umbrella term for its time-stretch engine — warp markers anchor moments of audio to grid positions, and the chosen warp mode decides how the algorithm fills between them. Simpler has Warp; Sampler does not.
wikipedia www.ableton.com lesson: w04-warp-modes
see also: complex_pro_warpbeats_warprepitch_warptexture_warptime_stretchingbib:ableton_live_12_manual

Signal path — 8 terms

bounce in placebounce_in_place
Ableton 11's 'Freeze + Flatten' on one click — takes a track's current output and replaces it with a committed audio clip, clearing the devices. Functionally identical to the classic tape-recorder bounce. In 12.3 it extends to Bounce Groups (bouncing grouped tracks to stems in a single gesture).
wikipedia www.ableton.com lesson: w10-variation-generation
see also: freezeconsolidatebounce_groupsbib:ableton_live_12_manual
busbus
A routing node in a mixer that combines multiple channels into one — typically for group processing (drum bus, vocal bus, master bus). In Ableton, Group Tracks are buses.
wikipedia
see also: groupinsert_send_returnbounce_groups
consolidateconsolidate
Cmd/Ctrl+J in Ableton — merges a selection of clips into a single new audio clip, warp-locked. Use it to commit an arrangement section before destructive edits. Preserves the underlying warp as the new source.
wikipedia www.ableton.com
see also: freezebounce_in_placebib:ableton_live_12_manual
freezefreeze
Ableton's render-in-place for CPU relief: the track's audio output is temporarily baked to disk; the devices are bypassed until unfrozen. Freeze alone keeps the devices live; Flatten discards them and commits the audio permanently. Burial's workflow leans on this.
wikipedia www.ableton.com
see also: bounce_in_placeconsolidatebounce_groupsbib:ableton_live_12_manual
gain staginggain_staging
Managing level at every point in the signal path so each stage gets the right input without clipping and without buried signal. With 32-bit float internals, clipping inside the DAW is not catastrophic — but analog-modeled plugins still respond to input level, so discipline still pays.
wikipedia
see also: dbfstrue_peakfloat_32
groupgroup
A folded set of tracks sharing a parent bus, with a single fader and insert chain. Ctrl/Cmd+G in Ableton. Groups can be nested; they are how you gate a whole bus from one sidechain or apply one master EQ to the drum family.
wikipedia www.ableton.com
see also: busbounce_groupsbib:ableton_live_12_manual
insert / send / returninsert_send_return
Insert: an effect placed in-line on a channel — 100% wet, the signal flows through. Send/Return: the channel taps a copy into an aux bus (send), that bus hosts the effect (return), the two are mixed back together. Reverbs and delays live on sends; EQ and compression live on inserts.
wikipedia
see also: pre_post_faderbusgain_staging
pre-fader / post-faderpre_post_fader
Whether a send is tapped before the channel fader (level-independent — classic for cue mixes and feedback routing) or after (level follows the fader — standard for effects sends). In Ableton each send has a Pre toggle.
wikipedia
see also: insert_send_returnbusgain_stagingbib:ableton_live_12_manual

Ableton specifics — 18 terms

Arrangement Mixerarrangement_mixer
Live 12.3 feature (⌥⌘M) — docks mixer strips beside the Arrangement timeline so you can ride faders and sends without tab-switching. Small UI change, meaningful for the bounce-and-arrange workflow.
wikipedia www.ableton.com
see also: bounce_groupsbounce_in_placebib:ableton_live_12_manual
Beats warpbeats_warp
Warp mode that slices on transients and retriggers each slice at grid — preserving attack, introducing audible tails or gaps between hits. Default for drum loops. Preserve 1/4 with Transient Envelope 0 gives choppy stretched segments. Fast, low-CPU, honest about its artifacts.

Live 12.3 feature — bounce an entire group track to a new audio track in one action, with optional Paste Bounced Audio back into place. Turns bounce-and-arrange from a manual ritual into a keyboard shortcut.

Capture MIDIcapture_midi
Ableton's retroactive MIDI recorder — it keeps a rolling buffer so you can play something you weren't recording and then grab it. In 12.3 the buffer no longer clears when a clip is playing back, which makes improvising over a generative patch actually survivable.

Chain Selectorchain_selector
An Instrument/Effect Rack's Zone control that routes MIDI or audio to different chains based on a macro value. Combined with Macro Variations (Live 12.2+), gives you recallable snapshots — an Autechre-style patch-flipper in a single rack.

Clip Envelopesclip_envelopes
Per-clip modulation curves (volume, filter, send, device parameter) that can run at a different length from the clip's audio loop. A 4-bar audio clip with a 7-bar filter envelope never repeats identically — cheap polymeter for free.

Complex Pro warpcomplex_pro_warp
Ableton's highest-fidelity phase-vocoder warp mode. Preserves formants at heavy pitch shifts (dial 0–100%), with an Envelope parameter that tunes window size — 64 for hats, 256 for bass. Softens kick transients as a side effect; nudge markers pre-kick or layer Beats mode to fix.
wikipedia www.ableton.com lesson: w04-warp-modes
see also: warpbeats_warptexture_warprepitch_warpphase_vocoderbib:ableton_live_12_manual
Follow Actions 2.0follow_actions
Ableton's per-clip logic for triggering the next clip in a track. 2.0 (Live 11+) added per-scene follow actions, probability distributions, and legato launching. Set 8–16 drum variants to Any with mixed probabilities — non-repeating drum tracks built from hand-crafted parts.

Macro Variationsmacro_variations
Recallable snapshots of a Rack's macro values (Live 12.2+). Eight macros, sixteen variations, one-click morph between them. Used as a compositional device this is how you do beat-switches from inside a single device instead of across tracks.

Live 12's non-destructive MIDI generation tools, chainable and re-runnable. Rhythm builds step-based drum patterns. Seed does polyphonic random notes. Shape draws monophonic contours. Stacks makes chords. Euclidean distributes pulses evenly — classic 16/5 hat patterns in one click.

Music theory — 4 terms

centcent
1/100 of an equal-tempered semitone — the unit of microtonal pitch. 1200 cents per octave. A 5-cent detune is the classic chorus/thicken; a 7-cent random offset per sustained note is what makes an Aphex pad shimmer instead of sit.

equal temperamentequal_temperament
A tuning system with equally spaced steps. 12-tone equal temperament divides the octave into 12 equal semitones of 100 cents each — the default assumption of Western music since roughly Bach's time. Every interval except the octave is a rational compromise; microtuning explicitly un-does those compromises.

ratio (just intonation)just_intonation
A tuning system where intervals are small-integer frequency ratios (3:2 for a perfect fifth, 5:4 for a major third) rather than equal divisions. Pure-sounding in a single key; wanders out of tune when you modulate. The ancestor system to equal temperament.

A scale is an ordered set of pitches that span an octave. A mode is a specific rotation of a scale — Ionian (major), Dorian, Phrygian, Lydian, Mixolydian, Aeolian (natural minor), Locrian. The friction-between-modes trick in 'the rub' is two voices landing in different modes of the same scale.
