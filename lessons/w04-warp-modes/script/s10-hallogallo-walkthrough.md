Walkthrough. [pause 200ms] Open the live set `w04-warp-ab-bed.als` if you have it. If you don't, build this in thirty seconds.

New Live set. Four audio tracks. Drag `hallogallo_drums.wav` from the stemforge render directory onto each track — same file, four copies. Session view. Four clips. [pause 200ms] Now we configure.

**Clip one — Beats mode.** Preserve Transients. Transient Loop Mode Off. Transient Envelope one hundred. This is baseline. Kick punch intact, snare punch intact, hats chattery on the tail if anything. Play it. It should sound almost identical to the source.

**Clip two — Complex Pro, default.** Formants one hundred. Envelope one-twenty-eight. Play it immediately after clip one. [pause 300ms] Listen for the kick. The leading edge is softer. The click is reduced. This is the pitfall slide in audio form. Do not adjust anything yet; just notice.

**Clip three — Complex Pro, corrected.** Same settings as clip two. But before you play — zoom in on the waveform. Find the first kick transient. Grab the warp marker sitting on that transient and drag it to the left, five milliseconds. Visually, the marker now sits just *before* the attack. Do it for every kick in the loop. [pause 200ms] Play. The kick has regained most of its bite. Not all. But most. Alternate approach — skip the marker nudging, duplicate the clip to a fifth track, set it to Beats mode, high-pass at eighty hertz, sum at minus-six. Same result, different route.

**Clip four — Texture, aggressive.** Grain thirty milliseconds. Flux seventy percent. [pause 200ms] Play it. The motorik pattern is gone. What you have now is a dissolving percussive cloud. The kick and snare are *somewhere* in the cloud but you cannot pin them down. This is Texture doing its job — not preserving the source, mutating it.

[pause 300ms] While you audition — watch the waveform zoom in Live 12. You can see the transient envelope change *visually* before you hit play. Complex Pro renders the waveform with softened peaks. Beats preserves the originals. Texture redraws the clip as a blur. Your eyes tell you what the mode will do; trust them as a first pass, your ears as the second.
