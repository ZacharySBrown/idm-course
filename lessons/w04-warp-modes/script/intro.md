IDM Week four. [pause 300ms] Warp modes as sound design — Complex Pro, Beats, Texture.

Last week we built drum racks out of one-shots pulled from Charles Wright. You have a rack. It triggers samples. Good. [pause 200ms] This week we look at what happens to those samples when Live's *Warp* engine gets hold of them.

The framing shift is the whole episode. The manual treats warp as a utility — a way to make a sample line up with your project tempo. That is true. It is also the least interesting thing Warp does. The modes — Complex Pro, Beats, Texture, Re-Pitch — each impose a different kind of violence on the audio. Choose carefully and you get a new instrument. Choose badly and you get a *rounded* kick drum that sounds like it went through a damp towel.

We will work on one stem. The drums from NEU's "Hallogallo," separated via stemforge. Motorik: four-on-the-floor kick, eighth-note hats, snare on two and four. Repetitive enough that artifacts reveal themselves inside four bars.

Halfway through, we take a detour to a studio in upstate New York called Tarbox Road. It is not a digression. It sets up the teaser for Week seven and pays off something we planted in Week two.

By the end, you should have rendered the same loop through three warp modes, written three sentences about the differences, and banked a chipmunk-soul render for Week nine. [pause 500ms] Let's go.
