One — the **Dorrough 610**. It is a multiband broadcast processor. Designed, originally, to keep FM radio stations from over-modulating their carrier wave — a compliance tool, built by engineers who would have been horrified to hear what Fridmann does with it. The distortion characteristic that occurs when you overdrive a Dorrough — which no broadcast engineer would ever intentionally do — is pleasant. It is punchy in a particular, aggressive way. The compression is multiband, so the low end gets its own treatment; the kick and the bass do not fight. Fridmann chose it because the artifact was musical. That is the whole Fridmann bass. Signal into a broadcast limiter, overdriven, printed to tape or to the multitrack, committed. From now on, when we say **Fridmann bass** — that is what we mean.
