# Tarbox Road Studios — a converted dairy in Cassadaga, NY

The teaser dropped one paragraph. Here is the payoff.

<term key="tarbox_road">Tarbox Road Studios</term> is a residential facility in Cassadaga, New York, built by producer <term key="dave_fridmann">Dave Fridmann</term> in 1997 in a converted dairy building. The published discography is not small: <term key="flaming_lips">The Flaming Lips</term> (*The Soft Bulletin*, *Yoshimi Battles the Pink Robots*, *Zaireeka*), <term key="mercury_rev">Mercury Rev</term> (*Deserter's Songs*), MGMT (*Oracular Spectacular*), <term key="tame_impala">Tame Impala</term> (*Innerspeaker* — mixed at Tarbox from Kevin Parker's 16-track Boss BR-1600), Sleater-Kinney, Mogwai.

The reason Tarbox lands in Week 4 is that the room's signal chain is the clearest living example of *sound design as signal-path choice*, the same pillar Warp modes sit on. We are using it as the worked example.
