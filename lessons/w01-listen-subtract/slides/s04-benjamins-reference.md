# Benjamins — your reference for twelve weeks

`benjamins.wav` lives in the stemforge root. This week stemforge has already split it into four stems via the `default --no-slice` pipeline. <Citation bib="stemforge_repo" />

You will revisit this file in W4 (warp modes, because the bass line warps interestingly under Complex Pro), in W8 (as a source for feedback-loop resampling), and in W12 (as the benchmark against which your own final two tracks get A/B'd).

The stems, in order of how useful they are to Week 1:

- `benjamins_drums.wav` — the rhythm grid, isolated. This is what you mute first.
- `benjamins_bass.wav` — the harmonic skeleton. Solo it and you will hear the song structure.
- `benjamins_vocals.wav` — the attentional anchor.
- `benjamins_other.wav` — everything melodic and textural the separator could not classify.

Load all four on adjacent audio tracks in Ableton. Leave them there for the week.
