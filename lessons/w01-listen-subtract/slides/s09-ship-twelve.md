# Twelve in twelve

One track per week, twelve weeks. No track is *the* track. The track is the *log*.

Constraint and recombination beats sound design. Completion beats perfection. A shipped three-minute sketch in Week 3 teaches more than six months tweaking a kick.

The pattern the finishing producers share:

- Kieran Hebden released *Three* near-unmastered because the mix was good enough and the idea was right. <Citation bib="tape_notes_140_four_tet" />
- Porter Robinson rebuilt "Get Your Wish" dozens of times over two years — the craft is re-shipping, not the first pass. <Citation bib="song_exploder_porter_robinson" />
- Burial on Sound Forge: *"Fuckit, I'm going to stick to this shitty little computer program."*

After each track, three lines: *what worked, what was hard, what to try next.* Over ten tracks, patterns emerge. <Citation bib="oblique_strategies_eno" />
