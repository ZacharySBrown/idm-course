Drop one stem. This is the oldest ear-training exercise in the book and it still works.

The premise: a mix is a set of simultaneous decisions, and your ear has been trained, by years of consumer listening, to fuse them into a single experience. The way to un-fuse is to remove one element at a time. Mute the drums. What remains? Mute the bass instead. What remains? The ear does not get better at hearing what is *there* — it gets better at hearing what is *absent.* That is the skill.

[pause 400ms]

On Benjamins, the order of operations this week:

First, play the full mix for fifteen seconds. Just listen. Note the hook — whatever part of the track grabs your attention — and note where in the frequency range it lives. Most students notice the vocal first. Some notice the bass. If the track is mixed well, both are true at once.

Second, mute *benjamins_drums.wav.* Play again. Here is the revelation a lot of students have in this exercise — *where does the time go?* With drums muted, most students suddenly realize that the bass was holding the tempo the whole time. The kick was reinforcing the one and the three, but the bass was the rhythmic spine. This is load-bearing to how hip-hop-influenced IDM works. It is the reason Dilla's tracks survive with the drums strange.

Third, un-mute the drums, mute *benjamins_bass.wav.* The harmonic skeleton disappears. What is left is a rhythmic shell with a vocal floating over it, and the ear starts asking for the missing low-end the way a tongue asks for a missing tooth.

Fourth, mute everything except *benjamins_other.wav.* This is where, for most tracks, the identity is hiding. The texture was doing more work than the arrangement hierarchy suggested. Ableton does not give you a "texture" bus; the separator doing its classification job is effectively creating one.

[pause 400ms]

Write one sentence per stem. Not an essay. One sentence each. If you cannot describe what a stem is doing in one sentence, you are listening too broadly.

The trained ear is the one that can hear the absent part.
