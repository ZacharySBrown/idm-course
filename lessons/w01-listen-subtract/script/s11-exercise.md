Ship the listening.

[pause 400ms]

The deliverable. Thirty seconds. No synths. No processing. This is a listening exercise and it stays a listening exercise. You are not making a track this week. You are making a *bounce* that proves you heard the track.

Steps.

One. Load the four stems on four adjacent audio tracks in Ableton. Drums, bass, vocals, other. Align everything to bar one. Check that all four play back together and they sum — roughly — to the original mix. If they do not, something is misaligned, and you should stop and fix it before going further.

[pause 300ms]

Two. Solo each stem for twenty seconds. Write one sentence, on paper, per stem. What it is doing rhythmically — the subdivision, the feel, whether it is pushing or dragging the grid. What it is doing harmonically — the note, the chord, the register. Not a paragraph. One sentence. If you find yourself writing more, you are describing instead of *noticing.*

[pause 400ms]

Three. In Arrangement View, place a locator at zero-zero and a second locator at fifteen seconds. From zero to fifteen, the drums track is muted. At fifteen, un-mute. The other three stems play through unchanged for the full thirty seconds. This is the A/B. It is the crudest possible A/B, and it is exactly the right crudity for this week.

Four. Bounce the result as a thirty-second stereo file, forty-four-one sample rate, twenty-four-bit. File name: *w-zero-one underscore your-name underscore mute-dash-a-b dot wav.* Lower case. No spaces. The file-naming convention is going to matter in Week Twelve when the mastering engineer asks for a consistent delivery format; you are going to start now.

[pause 500ms]

Deliverable: the WAV, plus your four one-sentence notes. Post both wherever the course Discord is going to live. That is Week One.

[pause 400ms]

No triumphant wrap-up. Go listen.
