Twelve in twelve.

The cadence this course assumes: one track per week, twelve weeks. Nobody expects the tracks to be good. Nobody expects any single one of them to be *the* track. The track is the *log.*

[pause 400ms]

Constraint and recombination beats sound design. That is the working philosophy of this course, and it is not original — it is cribbed from a hundred interviews with producers who actually ship. The ones who do not ship are the ones waiting for the right sound. The ones who do are the ones making the wrong sound work.

Three examples, because the principle lands harder with receipts.

[pause 300ms]

Kieran Hebden. Four Tet. Tape Notes episode one-forty, from twenty twenty-four. Hebden released the record *Three* near-unmastered, mixed partially on laptop speakers in hotel rooms between gigs, because the idea was right and the mix was good enough. He spends about forty minutes in that interview downplaying the Omnisphere presets he used — *forum consensus says he used "Voices of Africa" on a track, his actual answer is more interesting* — but the takeaway is that he shipped. Three records in the four years before that, two more in the four years after. Four Tet's rate of release is the skill.

[pause 400ms]

Porter Robinson. Song Exploder episode on *Get Your Wish.* Robinson rebuilt that track dozens of times over roughly two years. He describes the craft as the willingness to re-ship — not the ability to get the first pass right. The first pass is not supposed to be right. The twentieth pass is right because the first nineteen existed.

Burial, on the DAW question, which is a famous quote — *"Fuckit, I'm going to stick to this shitty little computer program, Soundforge."* The constraint was the recording program. Burial did not hold out for Ableton. He used what was there.

[pause 500ms]

After every track you finish — and you are going to finish twelve — write three lines in a physical notebook. *What worked. What was hard. What to try next.* That is the log. Over ten tracks, patterns emerge. You will discover your specific rabbit hole. Mine is always the kick. Yours, I do not know yet. You will find out.
