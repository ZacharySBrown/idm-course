The forty-five-minute rabbit hole.

Every new sound gets forty-five minutes. Kitchen timer on the desk — a real one, an actual timer, not a phone, because the phone is a cheating device this week — and when the timer rings you commit. You bounce in place, or you freeze the track, or you drag the MIDI clip to a new audio track and delete the source. The *commit* is the point, not the sound.

This is not a productivity hack. It is a workflow that assumes you have already lost the other version of this fight — the version where you are four hours deep on a kick and you hate the kick and you cannot remember why you started. Every producer who has shipped has lost that fight enough times to develop a rule about it. Forty-five minutes is the rule.

The second half of the rule — *separate the four mentalities into four separate sessions.* The mentalities:

*R&D.* Sound design. Patch-building. You build eight patches, save them, and you close the project. Nothing from this session gets used yet.

*Writing.* You can only use patches you already saved. No new synths. No new presets. If the patch you need does not exist, you put a placeholder in, and you write around the hole, and you make the real patch in the next R&D session.

*Arrangement.* Monitors *down.* Arrangement View full-screen. Laptop lid closed if possible. You are not making sounds; you are moving clips.

*Mixing.* A separate pass, after arrangement is locked. No new clips, no new MIDI. Only gain, EQ, compression, and the return sends.

Mixing *inside* a loop is the canonical intermediate-producer mistake. Berklee, Point Blank, EDMProd — every syllabus agrees. The loop pulls you into the fine-grained decisions before the coarse decisions have been made. You end up soloing a hi-hat at 2 AM to adjust its reverb send by half a decibel before you have decided whether there is a bridge.

Eno kept his Oblique Strategies cards on the desk specifically for this moment. Pull a card. *"What mistakes did you make last time?"* Usually the answer is: you opened this session without a locator, and your forty-five-minute timer is not running.
