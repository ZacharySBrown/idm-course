[pause 200ms] Dilla. And a defined term, right now, for the course: *Dilla time*. That is the phrase for the deliberate per-pad swing signature — the fact that on any Dilla pattern, the kick has one swing percentage, the hat has another, and the snare is dragged twenty to thirty milliseconds late. The bar is polyrhythmic hybrid, not sloppy. Per Charnas, *Dilla Time*, page references throughout chapter six.

[pause 300ms]

This matters for the vocal exercise because Dilla applied per-pad drift to chopped samples too, not just drums. When he built the beat on *Don't Cry* — from *Donuts* — he sliced a vocal phrase at one-thirty-second-notes, rearranged the slices, and then nudged individual slices off the grid by different amounts. The sample becomes unrecognizable but the micro-timing feels like a person played it. That is the whole Dilla move in one sentence.

In Ableton the recipe goes like this:

[pause 200ms] Drop the Hot Pants bounce on an audio track. Right-click, *Slice to New MIDI Track*. Set Slice to one-thirty-second. Not one-sixteenth. One-thirty-second. You will get roughly twice as many chains as you are used to. That density is the point.

Now rearrange the MIDI notes. Do not replay the original order — if you play the chains back in order you just get the source phrase. Scramble. Use the *Recombine Shuffle* transformation on the MIDI clip to break order preserving content. Add Ornament at one-sixty-fourth for two or three flams. The flams are small. You should be able to count them on one hand.

[pause 300ms] Then the per-pad part. Open each chain's Simpler. Nudge a handful of note *starts* — not all of them, a handful — by plus or minus fifteen to twenty-five milliseconds. Different amounts per chain. The kick-equivalent chain gets no nudge. The snare-equivalent chain — whichever fragment falls on beat two and four of your pattern — gets dragged twenty-five milliseconds late. The other chains sit somewhere between.

Bounce. Listen. Commit.

[pause 200ms] A/B against *Don't Cry* if you want a receipt for whether you landed it. The Charnas book describes the *Don't Cry* process in enough detail to reverse-engineer most of it. The micro-timing, you have to hear.
