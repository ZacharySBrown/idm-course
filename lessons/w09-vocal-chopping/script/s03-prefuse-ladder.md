[pause 200ms] Prefuse 73 first, because his method is the foundation for everything else in this lesson. If you understand the ladder, the other three methods are variations on it.

Scott Herren's rig on *Vocal Studies and Uprock Narratives*, 2001, and *One Word Extinguisher*, 2003: two Technics turntables feeding an Akai MPC2000XL feeding Pro Tools. Nothing else load-bearing. He rejected the computer-IDM aesthetic of the era — the Schematic, Miami crowd — and said in XLR8R, on the record, ~"I value the artistic limits imposed by the more primitive technology."~ Meaning: the MPC constrained him, and he liked being constrained.

[pause 300ms] The ladder, verbatim from how he described it:

One. Sample a two-to-four-second vocal phrase onto a pad.
Two. Use the MPC's Chop Shop to slice the phrase across sixteen pads, syllable- or phoneme-length.
Three. Finger-drum a new rhythm from those pads — not the original rhythm.
Four. Resample the performance onto a single new pad.
Five. Repeat steps two through four on the resample.

Step five is the part most producers skip. They do the chop, they do the finger-drum, they print, they stop. The ladder works because you keep going. By the third pass the source is genuinely gone and what you have is a performance of a performance of a performance. [pause 200ms]

In Ableton the verbs are: drop the stemforge bounce onto an audio track. Right-click, *Slice to New MIDI Track*. Slice to one-sixteenth, or By Transient — By Transient is closer to MPC behavior on spoken material. You now have a Drum Rack where each chain is one fragment. Record a new pattern using *Note Repeat*. Freeze the track. Flatten it. That bounce is your new pad. Load it into a fresh Simpler in Slicing mode. Slice again. Record again. Flatten again. Three passes.

Herren on timing — ~"I don't try to match bpms and shit."~ Do not quantize tight. If anything, turn off quantize during the finger-drum. The drift is the feel. [pause 300ms]

Layer doubled fragments at slightly different pitches for the *"eh-ah-oh"* micro-variation — Little AlterBoy on a chain, Formant plus-or-minus five, Pitch plus-or-minus two. Instant Prefuse.
