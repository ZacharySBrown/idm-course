[pause 200ms] Before we get to the exercise, a reference slide. The common signal path for all four methods this week, in order, in Ableton verbs. If you lose the thread, come back to this slide.

[pause 300ms]

Step one. Stemforge bounce. Load `build/audio/stemforge-renders/w09/hp_vocals.wav`. This is the LALAL isolate of the Hot Pants vocal. Freeze the track. Flatten it. The stemforge output is source-of-truth. LALAL is non-deterministic — we established that in slide two — so this file on disk is the canonical version. All downstream chops reference the flattened bounce.

Step two. Slicer front-end. Simpler in Slicing mode. Choice of slice resolution depends on method. One-sixteenth for chipmunk soul — you want fewer, cleaner pieces. One-thirty-second for Dilla — you want dense, micro-length fragments. By Transient for Prefuse — you want syllable-and-phoneme shape, not grid shape. Warp mode Re-Pitch when you are transposing. Classic mode when you want pitch-time coupling for SP-1200 style.

[pause 300ms]

Step three. Per-chain character. Little AlterBoy on individual chains in the Drum Rack. Not on the group. The whole point of layering doubled fragments at slightly different pitches is that each fragment drifts differently. Formant plus-or-minus five, Pitch plus-or-minus two. That is the Prefuse "eh-ah-oh" — three chains, three different settings, same phoneme source.

Step four. Group width. Once the chopping and per-chain character are in, bus the whole chopped-vocal group to a single track. Insert MicroShift on the group. Style II, Detune a hundred percent, Delay a hundred percent. The stack slide from earlier adds Auto-Tune at retune-speed zero on the same group for MBDTF character. Pick based on what you want the bed to be.

[pause 300ms]

Step five. The commit rule. Every time you print a pass, delete the unused source track. Delete. Not mute. Not archive. Delete. The ladder only works if you cannot retreat up it. This is the Mr. Bill rule from the compendium, the Burial rule in spirit, and the Prefuse rule in practice. Committed to tape.

[pause 200ms] One track in. One bounce out. One commit per pass.
