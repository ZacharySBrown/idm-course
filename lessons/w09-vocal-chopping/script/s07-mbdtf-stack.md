[pause 200ms] The MBDTF-era choral stack — *My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy*, 2010 — sounds like a big treated choir. It is not a choir. It is one phrase, tracked six to twelve times, detuned and micro-timed to be slightly off from itself on every pass. Then treated as a bed.

[pause 300ms] The recipe, in Ableton verbs.

Duplicate your chopped-vocal track six times. That gives you six passes of the same performance. On each copy, you are going to do two things. One: nudge the clip start by plus or minus five to fifteen milliseconds. Different amount per copy. This creates the micro-time smear. Two: detune the clip in the clip properties by plus or minus five or ten cents. Some copies flat by five, some sharp by ten, one or two untouched.

Now bus all six copies to a group. Insert *SoundToys MicroShift*. Style II — warmer, slightly compressed — Detune at a hundred percent, Delay at a hundred percent. That is the course-standard setting. Style I models an H3000 Preset 231, which is tighter. Style III models the AMS DMX 15-80, which goes wider and dreamier. Pick Style II for Hot Pants. Pick Style III if the vocal is slow and sustained.

[pause 300ms] On the same group, insert Antares Auto-Tune set to Retune Speed zero. This is the Mike Dean trick — the *Heartbreak* trick, from *808s and Heartbreak* onward. Retune Speed zero means every pitch is yanked hard to the nearest scale note with no glide. The effect on a chopped-vocal stack is a hard talk-box lock on top of the detune smear. It is the sound of MBDTF's bridges.

[pause 300ms] A caveat inline. Bounce the bed to a single audio track. Place it under the drums at minus ten to minus fifteen dB. Do not ride the fader. [pause 200ms] Commit, and move.
