[pause 200ms] Chipmunk soul. Course term. Kanye West, Just Blaze, and — earlier — RZA. The receipt is Just Blaze's Red Bull Music Academy lecture, which is archived on the Wayback Machine because RBMA's site is gone.

Blaze, paraphrased close to verbatim: ~"RZA started it in like 1996. RZA's style was closer to a sped-up loop. The way me, Kanye and Just chopped it, it was more — we'll get seven pieces of a record, put it back together, add a breakdown part and make the sample do the hook."~ [pause 200ms] Seven pieces. That is a specific number. It is also a specific method.

[pause 300ms]

Here is the Ableton part, and this is the direct callback to Week 4 on warp modes. Pay attention to which warp mode you use, because it is the difference between chipmunk soul and something that sounds wrong in a different way.

Drop the vocal phrase on an audio track. Set the warp mode to **Re-Pitch**. Transpose plus five semitones. Try plus four, plus six, plus seven. The range is documented.

Re-Pitch is the varispeed model. Pitch and tempo stay coupled, like a tape machine sped up. The artifact — the chipmunk — is the pitch *and* the tempo moving together. That is exactly what the original producers heard when they pitched a record on a turntable or on an MPC with the tuning knob turned.

[pause 300ms] If you reach for Complex Pro instead, Complex Pro will decouple the pitch from the tempo. It will give you a +5 pitch shift at the original tempo, which is technically correct and aesthetically wrong. Complex Pro sounds correct and wrong. Re-Pitch sounds incorrect and right. That is the whole lesson on this slide.

Now chop. Seven pieces, non-contiguous. Vowel fragment in piece one. Consonant attack in piece two. Tail of the phrase in piece three. Middle consonant in piece four. You are not chopping the phrase in sequence; you are chopping across it. Reassemble those seven pieces into a new four-bar hook. Make the sample do the hook — that is the line.

[pause 200ms] The A/B on the slide plays the same phrase at +5 under Re-Pitch and at +5 under Complex Pro. Listen once. You will not reach for Complex Pro on a chipmunk again.
