# DJ Premier — scratch the loop, then sample yourself

Premier on *Ten Crack Commandments* (`bib:premier_ten_crack_scratches`): *"That loop is me sampling my scratching by hand. It was me experimenting, and it sounded dope."* Not a breakbeat, not a horn. A scratched performance triggered as a drum.

The method, verbs only:

1. Perform a pass on a sound — turntable, MIDI retrigger, tape-stop, whatever has a hand on it.
2. Bounce the pass.
3. Load the bounce into a sampler and **trigger as percussion** — not melody, not loop.

Ableton for the Hot Pants vocal: record yourself dragging the clip-start warp handle live. Bounce that performance. Load into Simpler. Trigger from a drum pad under the kick.

Premier's rig was MPC60 + Technics 1200s. Method is not gear-dependent.
