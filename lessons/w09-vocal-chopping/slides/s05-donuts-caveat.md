# Correcting the Donuts SP-303 myth

Charnas, *Dilla Time* (`bib:charnas_dilla_time`): *Donuts* was primarily **Pro Tools + Akai MPC3000** (`bib:akai_mpc3000_manual`). The SP-303 and SP-404 arrived at the hospital late, after most of the record was tracked. The Smithsonian holds the MPC3000 as the instrument of record (`bib:smithsonian_dilla_mpc3000`).

The "made entirely on an SP-303 from a hospital bed" story is wrong. It mistakes circumstances for method. The method was the MPC.

Why it matters here: chasing SP-303 sound won't get Dilla's sound. Per-pad swing and 1/32 chops were MPC3000 behaviors. Ableton Simpler in Slicing + Ornament + nudged note starts is the closer analogue. The lo-fi crunch people credit to the 303 on *Donuts* is mostly vinyl source material plus MPC3000 12-bit bus summing.

Preserve this caveat when citing the lesson elsewhere.
