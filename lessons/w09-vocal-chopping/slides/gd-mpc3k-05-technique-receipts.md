# Technique continued — receipts and track-level evidence

- **"Get Dis Money"** (Slum Village, *Fantastic Vol. 2*) — 7-bar Herbie Hancock loop, kick refuses the downbeat (`bib:charnas_dilla_time`). The hats are the only element on the grid; everything else drifts.
- **"Runnin'"** (The Pharcyde) — Stan Getz *"Saudade Vem Correndo"* guitar pitched up a half-semitone, then LPF'd to extract a new bassline from the same source. One sample, two functions (`bib:charnas_dilla_time`).
- **"Don't Cry"** (Dilla, *The Shining*) — 1/32 vocal micro-chop. The course-canonical reference for the method taught in s04.

Why this matters pedagogically: Dilla didn't have groove templates. He had ears and a step editor. The per-pad swing setting on the MPC3000 was the *enabling* technology. Without it — e.g., on the <term key="sp1200">SP-1200</term>, which has one global swing — Dilla time as we know it is not possible.

That constraint is the whole reason this deep-dive exists opposite the SP-1200 deep-dive.
