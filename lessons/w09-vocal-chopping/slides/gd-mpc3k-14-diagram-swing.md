# Diagram — Dilla swing visualized on the 96 PPQN grid

The tick-level reality: the 16th grid marks 24 ticks apart; the snare pad's notes sit 3 ticks *before* the grid; the kick pad varies ±3 to ±4 ticks off-grid; the hats stay on swing-64% positions (every odd 16th pushed ~8 ticks late).

Close the diagram with the rule: **different offsets per pad, all in the same 4-bar pattern.** That is the single architectural fact that separates MPC3000 Dilla time from every SP-1200 record that came before.

At 90 BPM, the perceptual threshold for "late" is about 8–12 ms (roughly 1–2 ticks). Anything below that is "tight." Anything above ~40 ms starts reading as a separate event. Dilla's sweet spot: 15–30 ms off-grid per pad, direction varying.
