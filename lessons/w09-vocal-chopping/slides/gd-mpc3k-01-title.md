# Akai MPC3000 — the machine Dilla actually used

The <term key="mpc3000">Akai MPC3000</term>, 1994. Designed with Roger Linn. 16 velocity-sensitive pads, 16-bit 44.1 kHz sampling, SCSI, 760 KB base memory upgradable to 32 MB, 32-voice polyphony. The <term key="smithsonian_dilla_mpc3000">Smithsonian National Museum of African American History and Culture holds Dilla's personal unit</term> as the instrument of record (`bib:smithsonian_dilla_mpc3000`).

Forget the *Donuts* SP-303 myth — this is the machine that defined Dilla's sound from *Fantastic Vol. 2* through *The Shining* (`bib:charnas_dilla_time`). The SP-303/404 arrived late, after the record was already tracked.

What this deep-dive covers: the controls that matter for <term key="dilla_time">Dilla time</term>, the per-pad swing mechanic, the 96 PPQN timing grid, and how to approximate it in Ableton without lying to yourself about what's missing.
