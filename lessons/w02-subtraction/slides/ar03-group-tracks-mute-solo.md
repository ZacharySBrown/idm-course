# Group tracks, Track Activator, and the mute-backward workflow

Group tracks in Ableton are folders for tracks. `Cmd+G` on a selection creates a group. In the group header, the <term key="track_activator">Track Activator</term> (the green square top-left of the header) toggles the entire group.

**The Subtraction workflow, in six keystrokes:**

1. Select the Peak loop's tracks. `Cmd+G`. Group named "PEAK".
2. Drag-select across the Peak locator region. `Cmd+D`. Duplicates the region across the timeline.
3. Inside each non-Peak locator region, click individual track activators to mute lanes.
4. `Cmd+0` (zero) toggles the clip-level activator if you want to mute a single region without muting the lane.
5. `S` solos the selected track — used for A/B listening, not arrangement.
6. `M` mutes the selected track. `0` on the track header does the same.

**Distinction.** <term key="track_activator">Track Activator</term> (green square) is what Subtraction uses — it persists across the whole track. <term key="solo">Solo</term> (the S) is an audition tool only. Don't confuse them in your arrangement pass. `bib:ableton_live_12_manual`.
