# The six-minute locator template — density curve

The template from slide `s03` plotted as a density curve. The y-axis is active-track count (a proxy for arrangement density); the x-axis is time.

**Read the shape, not the numbers.** The curve starts low (Intro: two active lanes), climbs through A and B, drops sharply into the Reduction (one lane), spikes into the Peak (all lanes), and tapers through the Outro. The one-bar silence sits inside the Reduction as the curve's minimum.

**Why this shape and not another.** Handbook §5.3: the Reduction's job is to throw the Peak into relief. If the density curve is monotonic — always climbing or always falling — the Peak lands as predictable. The dip before the peak is what makes the peak feel *earned*. See the SVG on this slide for the template shape; overlay it on your own arrangement to compare.
