# An arrangement view, dynamics arc visible to the eye

A well-subtracted track has a shape you can see in the Arrangement View before you hit play. Density low at the edges, peaks around the two-thirds mark, one visible gap inside the reduction.

**What a good reference arrangement looks like on screen:**

- Lanes thinning toward the intro and outro (tracks muted → empty regions)
- A dense block in the Peak region where every lane is lit
- One narrow vertical gap — the one-bar silence — inside the Reduction
- A constant lane running edge-to-edge (the anchor)

**Why the screenshot matters.** Subtraction is partly a visual method. If your Arrangement View looks uniform from bar 1 to bar 144, the listener's attention will drift at the same rate. Shape on screen predicts shape in the ear. Handbook §5.3.
