# The Locators pane — where the template actually lives

<term key="locators">Locators</term> are timeline markers in Ableton's Arrangement View. They live in the scrub-area above the tracks. Right-click the ruler → **Add Locator** (or use the ▶ button in the transport toolbar). Rename inline.

**What the Locators pane buys you for Subtraction:**

- **Named sections that travel.** Locator names bake into bounces as chapter markers — when you export to WAV, a DAW with chapter-mark support shows them. Useful for A/B review.
- **Jump-to-section playback.** Number-pad digits 1–9 jump to the first nine locators. This is how you audition the Reduction without scrubbing.
- **Snap-to-grid placement.** Hold `Cmd` while dragging a locator to break snap if you want a one-beat offset. Otherwise it locks to the bar.
