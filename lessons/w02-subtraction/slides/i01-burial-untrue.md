# Burial, *Untrue* — the prehistory of the inverse drop

Before "drop" meant a festival moment, it meant <term key="inverse_drop">the place where a track removes its own drums</term>. *Untrue* (Hyperland, 2007) is the Rosetta stone. Listen to "Archangel" at 1:45: the kick-and-clatter pattern that carried the first minute is gone, and a sidechained pad is left holding the room alone. That gap is the <term key="reduction_section">Reduction section</term>, eleven years before producers started naming it.

**Why it reads as Subtraction.** Burial composed in Sound Forge without a grid — no MIDI, no Arrangement View, no locators. The whole record is regions placed on a timeline, <term key="track_activator">track activators</term> in spirit if not in software. When he wanted a section to change, he muted regions. That is the method. `bib:burial_soundforge_quote`.
