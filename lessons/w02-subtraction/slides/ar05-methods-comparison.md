# Subtraction vs Bounce-and-Arrange vs Skeleton — side-by-side

Three arrangement methods, three entry points. Subtraction is this week's. The other two are coming in later lessons — placed here so you know when not to use Subtraction.

**Subtraction.** Start from the densest Peak. Mute backward. Best when: you already have a strong sixteen-bar loop and finishing is the blocker. Worst when: your Peak is thin.

**Bounce-and-Arrange.** Bounce all stems first, then treat them as audio clips on a fresh Arrangement timeline. Cut, move, silence. Best when: you have too many options in the writing session and need to commit. Worst when: the writing itself is unfinished. Handbook §5.6.

**Skeleton.** Write only the sparsest version — anchor + one melodic idea + one percussive element — for the full six minutes, then build up. Best when: your Peak doesn't exist yet. Worst when: you already have a dense loop you love; Skeleton asks you to throw it away. Handbook §5.4.
