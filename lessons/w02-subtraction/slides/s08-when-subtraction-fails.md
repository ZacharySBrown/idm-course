# When Subtraction fails you

Subtraction is not the only arrangement method. Four more are coming — Variation, Skeleton, Resample-and-Mutate, Bounce-and-Arrange. Use Subtraction when it fits; do not force it.

**Subtraction is a bad fit when:**

- **Your Peak is thin.** Muting a thin Peak gives you a thinner A section. Fix the Peak first or switch to Skeleton.
- **The track wants a new idea at bar 80.** Subtraction forbids new elements. If the track is begging for a second motif after the Reduction, that's a Variation Generation session, not a Subtraction one. Handbook §5.4.
- **You cannot commit to a densest state.** If your Peak keeps changing, you're still in the writing mentality, not the arrangement mentality. Close the session. Come back with a bounced loop.

**Receipt.** Handbook §1 ("The two-mentality problem") and §6.1 are explicit: Writing and Arrangement are separate sessions. Do not mix them.
