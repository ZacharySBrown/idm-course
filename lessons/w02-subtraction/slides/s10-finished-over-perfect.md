# Finished is better than perfect — Rubin, Conte, and the kitchen timer

**The mantra, Handbook §6.1:** *"Good is good enough. Finished is better than perfect. Ship the track."* — Jack Conte via Rick Rubin (primary sources in `needs_bib:`).

**Operational rules for this week:**

- **45-minute time-box** on any single element. Physical kitchen timer. When it rings, commit.
- **Huang's half-decibel threshold:** adjustments below perceptual threshold = perfectionism. Stop.
- **Eno's `Oblique Strategies`** when stuck: *"Cut a vital connection."* *"Just carry on."* (`bib:oblique_strategies_eno`)
- **Bounce in Place** the Peak *before* muting. Frozen audio is expensive to un-freeze — that expense is the feature. `bib:ableton_live_12_manual`.

The goal this week is not a great track. The goal is a *finished* track. Great comes from doing this fifty-two times.
