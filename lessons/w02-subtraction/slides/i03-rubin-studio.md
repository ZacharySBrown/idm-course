# Rubin in the room — the philosophy is a workflow claim

The reason Rubin keeps appearing in an arrangement-method lesson is not the mystique. It is a specific operational claim: that a finished rough is worth more than an unfinished masterpiece, and that the fastest route to a finished rough is to remove, not add.

**What the photo is here to do.** Remind you that the person saying *"less"* spent forty years producing records that sound like *more*. Def Jam, Johnny Cash's *American* sessions, Slayer, System of a Down. The advice is not monastic — it is post-abundance. Rubin had every option available and the method is what he chose anyway.

**For the Subtraction lesson.** Rubin's frame: finishing *is* a compositional decision. Muting a track at bar 64 *is* the composition. Handbook §5.9.
