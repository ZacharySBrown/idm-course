# The 8-bar rule and the one-bar silence

**The rule — Rubin via every producer who ever shipped:** if you enjoyed a loop for eight bars, break it. The moment you want to hear it again is the cue to cut.

**How to break it inside Subtraction:**

- Mute one layer for bars 9–16. Re-enable for 17–24.
- Duplicate the drum clip, shift it back 1/16 for bars 25–32.
- Insert one bar of true silence at bar 48. No tail. No tape stop. A gap.

**Silence is compositional.** Rubin: *"Perfection is obtained when there's no longer anything to take away."* (Handbook §5.9; primary source flagged in `needs_bib:`.) Autechre's *Tri Repetae* and Aphex's *#3* both use 2+ bars of near-silence as structural punctuation. Handbook §5.8, §5.9.
