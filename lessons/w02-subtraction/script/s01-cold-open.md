Here is the pattern. You sit down at seven PM. You load a kit. You find a hat pattern you like. By nine PM you have an eight-bar loop that genuinely sounds *good*. You A/B it against a reference — a Four Tet track, an Aphex track, whatever — and it holds up. [pause 150ms] Then you do something strange. You add a reverb send. You swap the kick. You EQ the hat. You try a different bass preset. By one AM you have destroyed the good loop and you cannot get it back, and the track still does not exist, and you close the laptop angry.

[pause 250ms] *What happened there.* You were not mixing. You were not writing. You were trying to make the loop sound *finished* — when the loop was already finished, two pages ago. The *track* was the thing that was unfinished. And you were looking in the wrong place.
