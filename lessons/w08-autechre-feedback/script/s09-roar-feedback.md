Before we touch Roar — a short apology. [pause 300ms] The marketing calls it a saturator. It is not a saturator. It is *three distortion stages taped together with a feedback loop.* That is better than what the marketing says. That is why we are here.

Roar shipped in Live 12.2. The configuration for our purposes — MultiBand mode, three shaper stages, plus the feedback section. [pause 400ms]

Low band, below a hundred twenty hertz — Shaper set to Tube. Drive forty percent. Mix seventy percent. This is where the kick lives. Tube is musical at low frequencies, less brittle than Diode down there.

Mid band, eight hundred hertz to three kilohertz — Shaper set to Diode. Drive sixty percent. Mix fifty percent. This is where the snares and the body of the drums sit. Diode gives you the *rubbery* character that the spec attributes to Autechre's home-brewed FM drum synthesis — and I will flag that the home-brewed-FM claim is itself KVR and Elektronauts forum consensus, not officially confirmed by Booth or Brown.

High band, above three kilohertz — Shaper set to Shards. Drive twenty-five percent. Mix thirty percent. Shards is Ableton's aggressive option up top. It will tear the cymbals apart if you push it past forty.

Then the part that matters most — [pause 400ms] the feedback section. Time set to one-sixteenth. Amount at fifteen percent. Routed post-shaper.

Start at fifteen percent. Listen. Past twenty-five percent Roar begins to self-oscillate. Past forty percent it screams. The *interesting* region is between ten and twenty, where the drums start to smear into each other without losing their individual transients.

This is the Ableton-native approximation of what Booth described in *SOS* April 2004 — [pause 300ms] *stuff can go back in and back out as many times as we want it to.* The Shure Auxpander eight-by-eight rotary patchbay, with knobs controlling how much signal goes into each return, compressed into a single plugin.

A-B against the stemforge render — `w08-moanin-ambient-feedback`. You have a dry Moanin' stem and a wet ambient pipeline version. Run your Roar chain on top. Listen to how little it takes before the kit stops sounding like Blakey and starts sounding like a texture.
