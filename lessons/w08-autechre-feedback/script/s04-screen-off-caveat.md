A brief, honest flag. [pause 400ms]

You will hear this quote, probably in the same week you buy a Max for Live device — *"Booth had to turn the screen off to finish Confield."*

It is the kind of sentence that gets posted on Reddit with no source and racks up upvotes. And the content is *roughly* right. But the specific framing — that Booth turned the screen off *in order to finish Confield specifically* — is community paraphrase. It is not a direct quote from a dated interview that I can point you to.

What Booth *did* say, in *SOS* April 2004, was a more general statement about studio practice. [pause 300ms] *There's nothing better than turning the screen off and just going analogue. The screen has to go off. It's an illusion that totally pollutes what you're thinking and what you're listening to.*

That is a studio-practice statement. It is *not* a *Confield*-sessions statement. [pause 400ms] Somewhere in the WATMM folk-transmission chain, those two ideas got welded together and attributed as a single sentence. I am not going to pretend that welded sentence is a primary source.

The reason we flag it: this course tries to cite before it claims. If a thing is forum consensus, we say forum consensus. If a thing is a direct quote, we name the page. The credit for the distinction goes to Booth himself — his willingness on WATMM to correct people when they mis-attribute his own work is why we have any taxonomy at all.

The practical advice is still correct regardless. [pause 300ms] At least once per session, close the laptop lid. Route monitoring to speakers. Play your track. Write notes on paper. Your ears get authority back from your eyes.

~One honest "unconfirmed" beats three "it is said that."~ [pause 500ms] Carry that with you when you repeat the lore.
