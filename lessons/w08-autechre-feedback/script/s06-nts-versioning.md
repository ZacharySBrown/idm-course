That is seven years of continuous development on one patch family. [pause 400ms] Think about that as a *practice*, not a technical curiosity.

It is archival, version-controlled composition. It is the discipline of saving many states of a thing, revisiting them years later, and recombining them. A Max patch treated the way a software engineer treats a git repository. You do not throw away the v01 when you make v02. You commit, you tag, you come back.

Your Ableton translation, three concrete moves —

First, save your Live sets with version numbers. `w08_v01.als`, `w08_v02.als`, `w08_v03.als`. Every time you bounce a committed chain, save-as with an incremented version. Do not overwrite.

Second, Live 12.2 added Macro Variations inside a rack. Combined with the Chain Selector, you can store eight to sixteen recallable patch snapshots *inside a single device*. Use them. When a sound idea is working, freeze its parameter state as a variation. You now have the sound and also the ghost of the sound from thirty minutes ago.

Third — [pause 300ms] when a rack sounds correct, save it as a preset with a dated name in its own folder. `Roar_feedback_bus_2026-04.adg`. Raid that folder six months from now. This is how catalogs get built. Not from genius. From *filing*.
