Quick detour — because "wrongness as signature" is exactly what MGMT's "Kids" is. [pause 400ms]

In March 2007, at Tarbox, Fridmann tracked the vocal on a Neumann U47 and then assaulted the whole mix with, quote, *substandard 1990s compressors*. He never named which ones. The engineers who've tried to reconstruct it on Gearspace have not agreed. The point: the sound is load-bearing, and it came from breaking cheap gear on purpose, and you cannot buy it. You can only imitate the *posture*.

The connection to this week is direct. Autechre's feedback routing — the Shure Auxpander into the Mackie, signal passing back through itself until the drum stops being a drum — is the same idea as Fridmann slamming a vocal through a broken compressor. Both are *wrongness on purpose.* Both are committed to. Both are un-repeatable by a plugin preset.

Roar is your cheap-compressor. [pause 300ms] Not because Roar is cheap — it ships with Live — but because Roar's feedback section, past fifteen percent, is a *wrong* place to put a drum bus, and the wrongness is where the signature lives.

Turn the feedback up until the texture becomes load-bearing. [pause 200ms] Then *stop turning it up.* The failure mode here is not being too cautious; it is self-oscillating into a drone you cannot steer.

The MGMT lesson, the Autechre lesson, and the Fridmann lesson collapse into the same rule — ~commit to the wrong path and do not back out.~ [pause 500ms]
