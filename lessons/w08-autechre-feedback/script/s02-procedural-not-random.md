Let's unpack the distinction, because it is load-bearing for everything that follows.

Booth posted something on the WATMM *Confield* thread years ago that keeps getting re-quoted because it is the clearest he has ever been about the taxonomy. [pause 300ms] *Algorithmic just means procedural.* Then he named three tracks off the record and graded them.

*"parhelic triangle"* — [pause 200ms] *wasn't algo at all.*

*"sim gishel"* — *slightly.*

*"uviol"* — *a lot.* [pause 400ms]

But, and this is the part to underline, *"none of it's in the way you prob think of 'algorithmic' with loads of randomness. It was all planned out, with bits of it being executed after intervals etc — like a track but codified."*

Three tiers of algorithmic content on one record, and not one of them reaches for a random number generator. The system is deterministic. The composition is what happens when you tune the system's parameters and then *choose* which run to keep.

Here is why this matters for your session today. [pause 400ms]

If you build a process and it sounds different every time you hit play, you have not composed anything. You have built a *toy*. The track is whatever the toy did the one time you decided to hit record. That is a defensible position — plenty of artists work that way — but it is not what Autechre are doing and it is not what we're teaching this week.

The alternative: re-seed your rhythm generator. Lock the seed. Lock the density. Lock the steps. Now hit play. Now hit play again. Now hit play a third time. If the three outputs are identical bar-for-bar, you have a *piece*. If they diverge, you have a *generator*. Know which one you are holding.

The run is the composition. You are allowed to pick which run. [pause 300ms] You are not allowed to pretend the dice rolled themselves.
