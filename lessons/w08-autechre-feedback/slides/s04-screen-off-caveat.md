# Caveat — the "screen off" quote

You will hear this one everywhere: *"Booth had to turn the screen off to finish Confield."*

Flag: **community paraphrase, not a verified direct quote from a dated interview.** SOS April 2004 has Booth saying something adjacent — *"there's nothing better than turning the screen off and just going analogue… the screen has to go off"* — but that is a *general practice* statement, not a statement tied to the *Confield* sessions specifically. The fused version is WATMM folk transmission.

The practical content is still correct, and the discipline matters:

- At least once per session, close the laptop lid.
- Monitor from speakers.
- Write notes on paper about what the piece needs.
- Your ears get authority back from your eyes.

Preserve the caveat when you repeat the lore. **One honest "unconfirmed" beats three "it is said that."**
