# Draft 7.30 (2005) — the pullback

*Confield* expanded. *Draft 7.30* contracted. On purpose.

Booth, SOS April 2004 follow-up material and later WATMM AMAs: *Draft* was written *"using straight-up normal sequencers and samplers. It's written note by note, where we know exactly what we put on."* They refused to download new software during production.

Why this matters for you:

- Expansion without contraction is a trap. You become a tool-collector.
- After every generative-heavy track you ship, commit to one track built note-by-note with no Max for Live, no new devices, no rhythm generators.
- The constraint is the composition. If you enjoyed the loop for 8 bars, break it — Rick Rubin via the arrangement chapter.

**Every third track: no generators.** Compose by typing.
