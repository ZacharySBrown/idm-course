# Confield (2001) — what the patch actually did

SOS April 2004, Booth speaking directly: Max/MSP was acquired in 1997 and became the organising metaphor for the studio. *"Most of Confield came out of experiments with Max that weren't really applicable in a club environment."*

Three ingredients of the *Confield* sound, per the same interview and the WATMM follow-ups:

- **Navier-Stokes fluid-dynamics simulations** used as slow continuous modulators for filter, pan, pitch. Fluid math as an LFO replacement.
- **Nested analogue sequencers.** One sequencer's control voltage decides what step another sequencer plays. A third sequencer drives the second at a different rate. Output: quantifiable complexity, not noise.
- **20+ minute takes edited down.** The evening's job is curation.

This is composition by **selecting from a rule-bound output stream.** You are not writing bars. You are tuning a system and then choosing.
