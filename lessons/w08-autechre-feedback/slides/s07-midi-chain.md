# The four-commit procedural MIDI chain

This is the core exercise of the week. Built from Ableton 12 **MIDI Transform & Generate** tools per the Live 12 manual.

The chain, in order:

1. **Rhythm generator** — Steps 16, Density 55%, Division 1/16, Shift 0. Generate the raw grid.
2. **Recombine (Shuffle)** — re-orders the card deck. Commit the new MIDI.
3. **Time Warp** — breakpoint speed curve 2.0x → 0.25x across two bars. A snare roll that decelerates.
4. **Velocity Shaper** — draw a curve, apply. Kill uniformity.
5. **Ornament** — Length 1/64, Density low. Flams and mordents.

**Commit each step.** Right-click the MIDI clip → Freeze/Flatten, or apply the transform and keep moving. Between steps, drag a copy of the clip into a scratch track so you can revert.

You are performing four discrete compositional acts. Do not stack transforms and apply once — the edits become uncollectable.
