# Roar — the Ableton-native feedback matrix

Before we touch Roar: an apology. The marketing calls it a saturator. It is **three distortion stages taped together with a feedback loop.** That is better.

Per the Live 12.2+ manual, the IDM drum-bus configuration:

- **Routing:** MultiBand, three shaper stages.
- **Low stage (<120 Hz):** Tube, Drive 40%, Mix 70%.
- **Mid stage (800 Hz–3 kHz):** Diode, Drive 60%, Mix 50%.
- **High stage (>3 kHz):** Shards, Drive 25%, Mix 30%.
- **Feedback section:** Time 1/16, Amount 15%, routed post-shaper. Start at 15%. Past 25% it screams.

The feedback matrix is what approximates the Autechre Shure Auxpander patchbay routing in a single plugin. *"Stuff can go back in and back out as many times as we want it to"* — Booth, SOS April 2004.

**A/B the stemforge render.** `stemforge:w08-moanin-ambient-feedback` gives you `moanin_dry.wav` and `moanin_ambient.wav`. The wet is Moanin' pushed through the ambient pipeline; your Roar pass runs on top.
