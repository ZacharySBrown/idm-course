# What else the hardware can do

Not every Machinedrum move is a drum move. The UW ("user-waveform") revision of the Machinedrum shipped with sample playback — the thing becomes a 6-track sample instrument the moment you load a kit of non-drum samples.

Non-obvious uses, pulled from Elektronauts threads and Autechre live-set reconstructions:

- **Chord stabs on a drum track.** Load a 4-second pad sample into an E12 machine, p-lock pitch per step. The "drum" is now a chord sequencer.
- **Granular-adjacent playback.** UW machines with very short samples (<200 ms) and p-locked start point per step. You get a micro-grain stream that is still step-quantized.
- **Audio-rate LFO via Nord G2.** Route an audio signal from the Machinedrum into the Nord G2, use the G2 as an audio-rate modulator for a Monomachine filter. The drum becomes the modulator.
- **Monomachine as drum synth.** FM machine tuned low with a very fast decay envelope — the Monomachine kick is a DX100-style kick. This is how Autechre got the metallic/woody drum-synth sound without using the Machinedrum.

The rig is not six drums + six synths. It is twelve voices with interchangeable roles.
