# The M4L ecosystem — who ships what

Four communities between them produce most of the useful Autechre-adjacent Max for Live devices.

- **Dillon Bastan** — chaos, attractors, physics. *Strange Mod* (3D chaotic attractors), *Entanglement* (coupled pendulums), *Tree Tone* (L-system melodies), *Markov Variations*, *data.mod* (modulation matrix). Closest living analog to the Autechre patch aesthetic.
- **K-Devices** — step sequencers, probability, transforms. *ESQ, HEXO, TATAT, TTAP, MOOR, Twistor*. Ship for a price; polished. Industrial.
- **Max for Cats** — Ableton's ex-in-house team. *Bengal* (6-op FM — the FS1R emulation), *SQ8L* (8-bit wavetable), *DROID* (drum synth). Free-to-cheap.
- **Cycling '74 + Ableton first-party** — the stock M4L devices. *LFO*, *Envelope Follower*, *Shaper*, *Note Echo*, *Chord*. Not exciting individually; useful as building blocks.

Also worth naming: **Akihiko Matsumoto MODE 5.0** (modal harmony), **alkman Euclidean Sequencer Pro**, **Surreal Machines Magnetic/Diffuse** (tape + reverb), **Iftah Sting 2** (acid bassline generator), **POLYRHYTHMUS** (free polyrhythm engine).

Pick one per session. Tool-collecting is the Draft 7.30 trap again.
