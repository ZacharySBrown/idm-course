# s08 — Metallurgist sidechain

"The Metallurgist" is a track on Squarepusher's *Ultravisitor*, twenty-oh-four. Warp. It is a long, patient, seven-minute piece that moves through about four distinct sections and one of the cleanest sidechain designs in the catalog.

Jenkinson on the track: *"On 'The Metallurgist,' the bass drum is the sidechain source, and each time it drops, the dynamics for other instruments are being changed to permit a sense of fluidity."* [pause 500ms]

Read it again. *All* other instruments. Not just the bass. The kick does not just duck the sub, which is the standard dance-music move. It modulates the dynamics of the pads, the leads, the vocal, the noise beds — the entire mix breathes on the one. [pause 300ms]

This is sidechain *as composition*, not as mixing. The kick is not punching a hole for the bass. The kick is *keying the whole arrangement*.

Flagging a caveat inline: the source for this exact phrasing is attributed to Jenkinson but I could not find the specific print interview anchor. I have flagged it in the lesson's `needs_bib` array. The technique is unambiguous and audible in the track; the exact quote provenance needs confirmation from the bibliography-expander.

Ableton recipe. [pause 200ms] Call it the **Metallurgist bus**.

Step one. Route the kick to a return track named `kick-trigger`, pre-fader. No audio output. This exists purely to be a sidechain source that you control independently of the kick's volume in the mix.

Step two. On every other instrument track in the session — sub, pad, lead, vocal, noise — insert an **Ableton Compressor**. Set Sidechain to ON. Source: `kick-trigger`. [pause 200ms]

Step three. Vary the depth per track. This is the part most producers skip.

Kick to sub: six decibels of ducking, five-millisecond attack, eighty-millisecond release. This is the classic EDM move; it is table-stakes.

Kick to pad: three decibels, twenty-millisecond attack, two-hundred-millisecond release. Longer release means the pad breathes rather than stutters. This is where the Metallurgist sound lives.

Kick to lead: one decibel, fifty-millisecond attack, thirty-millisecond release. Barely audible. A glint, a shimmer — the lead pumps but you cannot tell why.

Step four, optional. **Glue Compressor** on the master bus, sidechain ON, source `kick-trigger`, one decibel of grab. The whole mix moves together. You will not hear it individually on any channel but you will feel it on the bus.

~The result.~ [pause 300ms] A mix that breathes on the one. Every instrument has the kick baked into its envelope. Nothing in this mix was *mixed*. Everything was *composed*.

One honest warning. This technique goes too far fast. Three sidechain relationships is compositional; seven is seasickness. Start with three. If it still sounds inert, add a fourth. Do not start with nine. [pause 200ms]
