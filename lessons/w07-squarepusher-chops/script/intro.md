# Intro

Week seven. Squarepusher. [pause 400ms]

The man who sequenced entire tracks in a single pass from a Boss DR-660 driving an Akai S950, refused to let anyone watch him work, and wrote his own DSP patches on a PC in an editor called VSIG — then transferred them into an Eventide Orville by SysEx because the hardware refresh was faster than clicking a mouse.

Tom Jenkinson is the subject. *Tape Op* issue eighty-nine, May 2012, Vijith Assar interviewing. That is the primary source and we will cite it constantly. [pause 300ms]

Twenty-eight minutes. Eleven slides. One exercise at the end: chop the Amen-style section from the Funky Fanfare drums Stemforge rendered for us, slice to MIDI at sixteenth notes, rearrange the hits in one pass with no undo, commit what you get.

~The last part is the point.~ The chopping is trivial; Ableton does it in one right-click. The discipline is the content.

Headphones. Notebook. Let's go.
