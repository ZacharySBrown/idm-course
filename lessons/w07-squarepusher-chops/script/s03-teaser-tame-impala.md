# s03 — Teaser: Tame Impala Boss

Side note. [pause 300ms]

Squarepusher's QY700 is the anti-computer in its purest form, but it is not the only one.

Kevin Parker walked into Tarbox Road in twenty-oh-nine with a Boss BR-1600 sixteen-track digital recorder his dad had bought him when he was sixteen years old, and handed it to Dave Fridmann. [pause 300ms]

Instead of laughing, Fridmann mixed *Innerspeaker* from it. The whole debut album. Tracked on consumer-grade prosumer kit by a kid in Perth, mixed at Tarbox through a Neve 88RS and a Dorrough 610.

Parker, afterward: *"There's no way I could've made the album sound as good as Fridmann made it sound."* [pause 400ms]

Source is the AudioTechnology *Lonerism* feature. We will cite it in full later this semester.

The rig is a constraint. The engineer is the respect for it. [pause 300ms]

Two artists. Two postures. Jenkinson refuses the computer because the screen pollutes his mental model of the signal path. Parker refused it because the BR-1600 was the only box he had learned on and he did not want to re-learn his workflow for a debut album. ~Same outcome — arrangement-bound, commit-forward, recall-hostile — from opposite motivations.~

File the Parker story. We revisit the Fridmann bass the next time it is relevant. [pause 200ms] Back to Jenkinson.
