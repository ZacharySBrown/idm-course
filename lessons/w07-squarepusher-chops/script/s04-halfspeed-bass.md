# s04 — Half-speed bass

*Hard Normal Daddy*, nineteen-ninety-seven. Warp Records. [pause 300ms]

If you have never heard a Squarepusher bassline, this is the album. Fretless tone. Unreasonable speed. And the swing — the swing is not a quantize setting. It is neither straight sixteenth nor dotted. It sits in a pocket that a human wrist does not actually produce.

That is because a human wrist did not produce it. A tape machine did. [pause 400ms]

Jenkinson, Tape Op eighty-nine, verbatim: *"In the late nineties, when I was still recording on multitrack tape, I did a lot of stuff by recording at half speed. What I'd do is run the tape machine at seven and a half IPS and pitch everything down an octave, play it at half speed so that the tracking and timing issues would diminish once I put it back to fifteen IPS. It completely interferes with human fallibility regarding timing. The whole thing just goes unnaturally tight."* [pause 500ms]

Tape chain of record for this era: Fostex M80 into a Tascam MSR16, half-inch 16-track. IPS means inches per second — the speed the tape runs past the heads. Fifteen IPS is the standard mastering speed. Seven and a half is half that. Run at half, record down an octave and at half tempo, then flip back. Everything doubles in frequency. Everything halves in duration. Your sloppy playing becomes tight. Your wide timing becomes bionic.

Ableton translation. [pause 300ms]

Step one. Halve the project tempo. If the track lives at 160 BPM, set it to 80. [pause 150ms]

Step two. Record your bass part — MIDI or a real instrument miked in — an octave down, slowly, against the 80 BPM click. Take your time. Play it like you are drunk. Whatever.

Step three. Return the tempo to 160. Warp the clip **Complex Pro**, Formants on. Complex Pro preserves harmonic character; Formants-on avoids chipmunk artifacts on instrumental timbre.

Step four. The pitch snaps up an octave. The tracking tightens. The swing, the per-note micro-timing, remains. You get the bionic wrist without owning tape.

One caveat. Complex Pro is CPU-heavy. Bounce in place and delete the source once you are happy. ~Recall-hostile, remember.~

The deeper point is philosophical. The half-speed trick is not a post-processing technique. It is a *performance constraint baked into the signal path*. You play a different instrument when the click is half speed. Your choices are different. Your rhythmic intention is different. The tape is not warming the bass. It is negotiating with your wrist.

This is the Fridmann bass logic we flagged back in Week 4. The constraint is in the chain, not in the plugin.
