# Side note — the other anti-computer in the room

Side note — Squarepusher's QY700 is the anti-computer in its purest form, but it is not the only one. Kevin Parker walked into Tarbox Road in 2009 with a Boss BR-1600 sixteen-track digital recorder his dad had bought him when he was sixteen and handed it to Fridmann. Instead of laughing, Fridmann mixed *Innerspeaker* from it. Parker, afterward: *"There's no way I could've made the album sound as good as Fridmann made it sound."* The rig is a constraint; the engineer is the respect for it. <Citation bib="audiotechnology_lonerism_feature" />

Two artists, two postures. Jenkinson refuses the computer because the screen pollutes the mental model. Parker refused it because the BR-1600 was the box he had learned on. Same outcome — arrangement-bound, commit-forward, recall-hostile — from opposite motivations.

File the Parker story. We revisit the Fridmann bass next time it is relevant.
