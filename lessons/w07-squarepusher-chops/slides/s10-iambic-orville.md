# Iambic 9 Poetry — when the Orville fights the grid

*Iambic 9 Poetry* (2004): roughly 78 BPM, D major feel (D/Bm/G/F♯m), live fretless-style bass from Jenkinson, likely live drums for the first half. The primary processor is the **Eventide Orville**.

Jenkinson is self-taught in DSP via Curtis Roads' *The Computer Music Tutorial* (MIT Press, 1996). <Citation bib="roads_computer_music_tutorial" /> The Orville patches are his own, written in **VSIG**, Eventide's modular-patch editor — programmed on a PC and transferred via SysEx to the hardware. <Citation bib="tape_op_89" />

The track matters here for two reasons:

- **Live instrumentation is not forbidden.** The Squarepusher catalog is not all stuttered breaks. Fretless bass through a custom Orville patch is still a Jenkinson track. The signature is the signal path, not the tempo.
- **Your Max for Live patches are your VSIG.** Next week (Autechre) we sit with that idea. For now, file it: building the instrument is part of the composition.

For us, this week: stay on the breaks. Iambic 9 Poetry is the reminder that once you can chop an Amen, you can also play over one.
