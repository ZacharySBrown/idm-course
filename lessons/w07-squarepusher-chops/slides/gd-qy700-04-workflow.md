# How Jenkinson tracked Go Plastic on it

Step-by-step, reconstructed from Tape Op #89. <Citation bib="tape_op_89" />

1. **Patterns, not songs first.** Each pattern is a 2- or 4-bar loop programmed into the step grid. Jenkinson builds ten to twenty patterns before chaining any of them.
2. **No real-time play-in.** Numeric entry only. The notes go in as values, not as performance data. The blind-numeric entry is the anti-second-guess tool.
3. **Per-step velocity curves.** Once pitches are in place, he walks the velocity editor and hand-draws per-step dynamics. This is the "program like a player" layer.
4. **Pattern chain = arrangement.** Patterns are chained into a song. Rearrangement is a chain edit, not a piano-roll move.
5. **External voices assigned per track.** TX81Z on ch 1, FS1R on ch 2, S6000 on ch 3–10, internal AWM fills the rest.

No screen. No undo history. No view of the whole song at once. The mental model is the score.
