# Amen break — chopped in the S950, rebuilt in Simpler

The **Amen break** — the drum break from "Amen, Brother" by The Winstons (1969) — is the spine of drum-and-bass and of Squarepusher's early catalog. On *Big Loada*'s "Come On My Selector" (1997), the Amen is chopped, re-pitched, ghost-reversed inside an **Akai S950** sampler, triggered from a **Boss DR-660** drum machine. <Citation bib="tape_op_89" />

Jenkinson on the break: *"You can pitch it up, down, stretch it, reverse it, it does everything. The Amen break is very sonically rich, very spectrally dense."*

The S950's pitch-shift aliases. That aliasing is part of the D&B aesthetic — a crude repitch is a feature, not a defect.

## Ableton translation

1. Drop `build/audio/stemforge-renders/w07/ff_drums.wav` onto an audio track. <Citation bib="stemforge_repo" />
2. Right-click the clip → **Slice to New MIDI Track** → Slice to: **1/16**, Slicing Preset: **Built-In**. <Citation bib="ableton_live_12_manual" />
3. Each pad in the resulting Drum Rack is one 1/16 of the break. Re-pitch individual pads by ±7 semitones from the Simpler tab for the S950-alias flavor.
4. Arm a new MIDI clip. Finger-drum a new pattern. Commit.
