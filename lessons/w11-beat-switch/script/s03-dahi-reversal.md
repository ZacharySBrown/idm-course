[pause 300ms] The four-step recipe in Ableton —

[pause 150ms] Step one. Drag any two-bar clip onto an audio track. Right-click. **Reverse.** The clip reverses in place.

[pause 200ms] Step two. Insert **Auto Filter.** Low-pass twenty-four. Frequency four hundred hertz. Resonance zero-point-three. LFO sync at one-half-note. LFO amount sixty percent. What this does — you get a slow breathing filter that opens and closes across the bar. The reversed tail of the sample now swells in and out of audibility.

[pause 200ms] Step three. SoundToys **Crystallizer** after the filter — `bib:soundtoys_crystallizer_manual`. Preset *Reverse.* Mix thirty-five percent. This adds a second layer of reversed grains on top of the already-reversed clip — the density builds without adding a second sound source.

[pause 200ms] Step four. Bounce the whole track to a new audio clip. Delete the source. You have the seed for the pre-switch section of your arrangement. The dry original version of the sample is the post-switch section.

[pause 300ms] The reason this matters — when you reverse a sample, the attack envelope becomes a swell. Nothing hits the listener on the one. The ear orients to the *tail* of each phrase. When the dry, forward version enters at bar seventeen or thirty-three, that forward attack is the switch. You did not need a riser. You set up the switch by teaching the ear to expect swells — and then you broke the teaching.

[pause 300ms] This is the cheapest beat-switch setup in this lesson. One clip. Two plugins. One bounce. If all you do this week is the reversal trick, you will have shipped the exercise.
