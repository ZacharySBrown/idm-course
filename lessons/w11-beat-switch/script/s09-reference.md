[pause 300ms] Four tracks to rip apart this week. Not a passive listen. Active analysis.

[pause 300ms] One. *Kendrick Lamar — Money Trees,* 2012. The Beach House *Silver Soul* reverse is the intro. The beat opens up around one-fifty-five. No riser on the switch. `bib:pitchfork_dj_dahi_money_trees` for Dahi's own account of how it was built. `bib:whosampled_db` for the *Silver Soul* source.

[pause 300ms] Two. *J Dilla — Get Dis Money,* from *Welcome 2 Detroit,* 2001. The sample is Herbie Hancock, *Come Running to Me.* The sample implies one mode. Dilla's bassline sits in a different one. That is the rub, distilled. Dan Charnas, *Dilla Time,* `bib:charnas_dilla_time,` chapter fourteen, on the counterpoint habit.

[pause 300ms] Three. *Burial — Archangel,* from *Untrue,* 2007. The one-thirty moment — the kick walks out, the vocal stutters carry the weight. `bib:burial_soundforge_quote` for Bevan's toolchain. Archangel-specific production notes are still in `needs_bib:` on this lesson.

[pause 300ms] Four. *Aphex Twin — Come to Daddy (Pappy Mix),* 1997. The companion *(Mummy Mix)* is the textural cousin. Both are inverse-drop precedents — Aphex arrived a decade before Burial. Burial codified the move. Aphex invented it.

[pause 300ms] How to listen — three passes on each track.

[pause 200ms] Pass one. Full track, normal volume. Just listen. Write down where you think the switch moment is, in bars and seconds.

[pause 200ms] Pass two. Solo the kick channel in your head. Track the kick across the whole song — when is it present, when is it absent. On *Archangel* you will hear the kick disappear in a specific window. Measure it.

[pause 200ms] Pass three. Mute the kick in your head. Listen to what is carrying the arrangement *without* the kick. This is the hardest of the three passes. Your attention wants to follow the drums. Drag it off.

[pause 300ms] Rick Rubin — `bib:rick_rubin_creative_act,` on listening away from the center — the part of the mix that you are not paying attention to is usually the part that is doing the work. Train yourself to listen there.

[pause 300ms] These are your four reference tracks for the exercise. Do not open Ableton until you have listened to at least two of them with the kick-hunting discipline.
