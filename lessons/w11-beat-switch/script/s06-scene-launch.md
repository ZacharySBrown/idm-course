[pause 300ms] Ableton surface. The cleanest way to perform a beat switch in Live is Session View scene launch with global quantization set to one bar.

[pause 300ms] Setup — open your track in Session. You probably have your clips laid out in Arrangement already. That is fine. Copy your current pre-switch section into Scene 1. Drum Rack A, bass pattern A, whatever sample loop is running, your effect sends armed.

[pause 200ms] Build Scene 2. This is the post-switch — Drum Rack B with a different kit, bass pattern B in a different feel or a different mode, a different loop source if you are doing a full TDE-style flip. Make it *different* on one axis. Straight to triplet. Dense to sparse. Same-source to different-source. One axis.

[pause 300ms] The key setting — top of the Live window, the Global Launch Quantization menu. Set it to **1 Bar.** This is the whole point. When you click Scene 2 mid-bar, Live holds your click until the next downbeat, then fires Scene 2 on the one. The switch lands clean. Zak-Handbook Ableton surface, confirmed in `bib:ableton_live_12_manual` — *Launching Clips and Scenes.*

[pause 200ms] Arm Arrangement Record. Click Scene 1. Let it run eight to sixteen bars. Click Scene 2. Live records the switch into your Arrangement timeline as if you performed it live. Which you did.

[pause 300ms] Then refine in the timeline — nudge clips, draw in the automation around the switch point, drop a reverse cymbal half a bar before. The Session performance is the skeleton. The Arrangement edit is the detail pass.

[pause 300ms] Caveat — the crossfade tail. If Scene 1 has a long reverb send on the vocal or a one-bar delay on the snare, those will bleed into Scene 2 after the switch. You have two choices and both are musical.

[pause 200ms] Choice one — kill the bleed. Add a clip envelope on the effect return that mutes the send on the last beat of Scene 1. The switch is surgical.

[pause 200ms] Choice two — keep the bleed. The reverb tail becomes a *sonic fingerprint* that links the two sections — the Scene 1 vocal ghosts under the Scene 2 intro. This is how Burial handles most of his transitions — he does not edit the tails out.

[pause 300ms] Neither choice is sloppy. Pick one on purpose.
