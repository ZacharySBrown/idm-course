[pause 300ms] If you stack eight-chain Instrument Racks on both sides of the switch — pre-switch and post-switch — and each rack has three reverbs and a Granulator, your session will stutter. Freeze works, but Freeze does not let you A/B across Session scenes.

[pause 300ms] The discipline — four steps.

[pause 150ms] One. Build and dial in your pre-switch section. Bar one through bar thirty-two. Make it the best version of itself. Automation. Panning. EQ. All of it. Bounce it in your head.

[pause 150ms] Two. Commit the pre-switch. Live 12.3 ships **Bounce Groups** — right-click a group track, Bounce. One stereo stem, or split into constituent tracks. `bib:ableton_live_12_manual` — *Freeze and Flatten.* If you are on older Live, Freeze plus Flatten does the same thing track by track.

[pause 150ms] Three. Delete the source tracks — or group-disable them with the green Activate button — so Live stops spending CPU on racks you are not hearing. Save a new version of the set. `w11_trackname_v02.als.` Always save as a new version, never overwrite.

[pause 150ms] Four. Now build your post-switch chain. The CPU you killed in step three is the CPU you just got back.

[pause 300ms] This is the studio-rat half of the Burial Bounce-and-Arrange method — `bib:burial_soundforge_quote` for the toolchain quote, Zak-Handbook section eight for the method framing. Commit, then keep moving.

[pause 300ms] Honest cost — once you bounce the pre-switch, recalling it is slow. You cannot tweak a reverb tail on a committed stereo stem. You have to reopen the old version of the set, re-render, replace the clip. That is friction.

[pause 200ms] The rule is *commit, then switch.* Not *switch, then commit.* If you think the pre-switch still needs revision, do not bounce yet. Bounce when you are bored with the pre-switch — not when you are still excited by it. Rick Rubin's old line, from `bib:rick_rubin_creative_act,` page ninety-three ish, paraphrasing — *if you are enjoying the loop on bar eight, break it on bar nine.* Same mechanic.

[pause 300ms] Ship the first thirty-two bars before you start the next thirty-two.
