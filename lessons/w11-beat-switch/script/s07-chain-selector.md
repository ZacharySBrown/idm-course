[pause 200ms] The Chain Selector exposes a single macro knob that routes MIDI or audio through one of up to sixteen nested chains. You wrap your bass synth, or your drum kit, or your sample-chopper in a rack. You build three to eight variants as separate chains. The Chain Selector macro picks which chain is active. `bib:ableton_live_12_manual` — *Instrument Rack, Chain Selector.*

[pause 300ms] Live 12.2 added **Macro Variations.** Snapshots of every macro position on a rack, saved as named presets on the Macro bar. You can store "Verse A," "Switch B," "Outro C" as three snapshots on a single rack and recall any of them instantly. This is the feature that turns Chain Selector from a live-performance tool into an arrangement tool.

[pause 300ms] Workflow — four steps.

[pause 150ms] One. Build one Instrument Rack for your bass. Three chains. Chain one — clean sine through a low-pass filter. Chain two — FM growl with medium feedback. Chain three — a bit-crushed square wave through Decapitator.

[pause 150ms] Two. Drag Chain Selector onto Macro 1. Position the macro at chain one. Click the Macro Variations tab on the rack. Store the current setting as Variation "A."

[pause 150ms] Three. Change Chain Selector to chain two. Adjust filter. Store as Variation "B."

[pause 150ms] Four. In Arrangement, right-click your bar-thirty-three boundary, **Insert Locator,** label it *Switch.* Automate Macro 1 to snap from the Variation A position to the Variation B position at that locator. The rack reconfigures on the downbeat. The bass patch flips. The track does not stop.
