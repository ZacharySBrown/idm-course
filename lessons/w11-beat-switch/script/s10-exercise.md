[pause 400ms] The exercise.

[pause 300ms] Step one. Find it. Open your week's in-progress track. Scrub through the whole thing. Identify the dullest eight-bar section. If nothing is obviously dull, pick the section you are most tempted to skip when you listen back on the walk home. The section where your hand drifts toward the fast-forward. That is the one.

[pause 300ms] Step two. Pick one move. *Not two. One.*

[pause 300ms] Option A. TDE beat switch at bar four. Replace the drum kit and pattern for bars five through eight. Straight feel to triplet, or dense to sparse, or same source to different source. Use Session scene launch with one-bar quantize — slide six — or automate a Chain Selector macro variation — slide seven. Either surface works. Pick whichever is already open in the session you are in.

[pause 300ms] Option B. Inverse drop at bar four. Mute the kick for bars five through eight. Replace with four elements — pitched vocal stutters at one-thirty-second intervals, vinyl crackle or rain foley pushed to minus-nine decibels, a sub drone one octave below where the kick used to be, and a reverse cymbal on the bar-four downbeat. No white-noise riser. Slide five, the four-element recipe.

[pause 300ms] Option C. The rub. Keep the drums. Mute your current bassline. Write a new two-bar bass countermelody in a different mode than the sample or pad you have running in that section. Not a wrong note. A different mode — modal friction, not dissonance. Loop the new bass under the eight bars. Slide four.

[pause 300ms] Step three. Bounce it.

[pause 200ms] Render a sixty-to-ninety-second WAV of your new version — the eight-bar section plus enough context on either side for the ear to orient. Render a second ten-second clip of the original, un-switched, un-rubbed, un-inverted, as the A/B reference.

[pause 300ms] Deliverable — two files.

[pause 150ms] `w11 underscore trackname underscore switch or inverse or rub dot wav.` That is the new version.

[pause 150ms] `w11 underscore trackname underscore original dot wav.` That is the A/B.

[pause 300ms] No mastering. No second-guessing. No adding a fourth element. The exercise is thirty to sixty minutes. If you spend two hours on it you are gold-plating and you should stop.
