# Untrue — the record that codified absence

- Burial (William Bevan), *Untrue* (Hyperdub HDBCD002, 2007). *Archangel* is track 2.
- Burial does not do press photos. The album cover — a red-tinged figure on black — is effectively the only public visual for the project. Use the cover when you need an image. `bib:burial_soundforge_quote` is the primary-source interview we have.
- Bevan on tools: *"Fuckit, I'm going to stick to this shitty little computer program, Soundforge."* This is not humility. This is a commit-to-a-constraint declaration — no plugins, no VSTs, waveform-level edits in Sound Forge.
- Why that matters for the <term key="inverse_drop">inverse drop</term>: Sound Forge does not have sidechain compression, does not have a mixer with sends, does not have automation lanes. If you want energy to *drop*, you mute a clip. The inverse drop is a **tool-shaped move** — it is what a drop looks like when the only verb you have is "cut."
