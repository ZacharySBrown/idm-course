# Aphex — Come to Daddy (Pappy Mix) — the earlier precedent

- Aphex Twin, *Come to Daddy*, *(Pappy Mix)* — Warp WAP94, 1997. Ten years before *Untrue*.
- The *Pappy Mix* (and its counterpart the *Mummy Mix*) does the inverse-drop move: at the point the hardcore side of the single would crank, the drums pull back and a textural pad/sub carries the passage. Richard D. James was there first; Burial codified it.
- **What Aphex did differently from Burial:** James's textural replacement was synthesized pad + sub; Bevan's replacement was sampled foley + pitched vocals. Different sources, same structural move.
- This is a generally useful lesson — the structural move is language-independent. Once you can hear the shape ("kick walks out at peak"), you will hear it in Dilla (end of *Don't Cry*), in Mount Kimbie (*Before I Move Off*), in FKA Twigs' *Two Weeks* second verse. It pre-dates Burial and outlasts him.
- **Receipt discipline:** `compass_artifact_wf-efadee41` §5 names both Aphex and Burial as co-originators of the inverse drop in IDM discourse; no single interview proves Aphex did it first by intent, only the chronology of release.
