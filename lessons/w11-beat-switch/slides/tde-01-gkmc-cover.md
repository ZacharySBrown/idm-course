# good kid, m.A.A.d city — the album that proved the switch

- *good kid, m.A.A.d city* (Top Dawg / Aftermath / Interscope, 2012). Executive production: Dr. Dre, Anthony Tiffith. Musical director credit effectively: Sounwave.
- This is the record where the <term key="tde_beat_switch">TDE beat switch</term> becomes a stated compositional move, not a novelty. *Money Trees*, *m.A.A.d city*, *Swimming Pools (Drank) — Extended Version*, and *Sing About Me, I'm Dying of Thirst* all switch pockets mid-track without a transition.
- The production credit distribution matters: DJ Dahi produces *Money Trees*; Hit-Boy does *Backseat Freestyle*; THC produces *m.A.A.d city* (with Sounwave co-producing). The switch is not one producer's signature — TDE adopted it as a house grammar.
- Sample-receipt check via `bib:whosampled_db`: *Money Trees* samples Beach House *Silver Soul* (2010), reversed. *m.A.A.d city* samples Ice Cube *Bird in the Hand* on the switch side. The switches are often also *sample* switches.
