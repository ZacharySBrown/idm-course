# The thesis — constraint is the generator

Week 2 was densest-first, mute-backward. This week is the inverse. **One loop, five duplicates, five prescribed mutations.** You do not pick the mutations. You apply them and ship.

Lineage: Finish More Music calls it "spawn from the same seed." <Citation bib="finish_more_music_variation" /> Spec A §5.4 calls it Variation Generation. Eno's *Oblique Strategies* — *"Honor thy error as a hidden intention"* — is the same idea with different packaging. <Citation bib="oblique_strategies_eno" />

**Mechanically:**

1. Duplicate your 16-bar loop five times along the Arrangement timeline.
2. Apply one mutation per duplicate — drum swap, bass seed, filter sweep, reverse, new layer.
3. Reorder for arc. Often 3–1–2–5–4 reads better than 1–2–3–4–5.
4. Bounce. Move on.

Five sections share DNA because they started identical. Coherence is baked in at the duplicate step.
