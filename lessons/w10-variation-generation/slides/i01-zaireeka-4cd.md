# Zaireeka — the constraint that broke, then worked

*Zaireeka* (Warner Bros. 9 46804-2, October 28 1997) shipped on four separate CDs meant to play simultaneously on four stereos. The listener assembles the mix. <Citation bib="zaireeka_wikipedia" />

Fridmann and the Lips tracked it at Tarbox Road — the studio's first project. <Citation bib="fridmann_davefridmann_com" /> The early sessions assumed they could split a normal four-piece arrangement across four discs. Halfway through they discovered the constraint forced them to write differently — <term key="phase_vocoder">interlocking rounds</term>, staggered vocals, deliberate gaps on one disc filled by another.

Months of the first attempt were scrapped.

The record that shipped is the second draft. The first draft is the lesson.
