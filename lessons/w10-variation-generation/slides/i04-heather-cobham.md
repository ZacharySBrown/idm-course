# Billy Cobham, *Spectrum* (1973) — why Heather survives chopping

*Spectrum* (Atlantic SD-7268, 1973). Cobham's solo debut. *Heather* sits in the ballad slot — long-phrased, wide-swing drumming, the kit sitting *behind* the grid rather than on it.

Two-bar chunks from Heather preserve the off-grid feel without locking you into Cobham's kit voicing. That is why the stemforge recipe stratifies at 2 bars, not 4 or 8.

The unexpected transferable property — when you drop an `a_*.wav` chunk into a <term key="follow_actions">Follow Actions pool</term>, the off-grid swing survives even through Live's warp engine, provided you set Warp mode to <term key="complex_pro">Complex Pro</term> with Preserve Transients off. Beats mode will quantize the swing back onto the grid. Week 4 receipts apply.
