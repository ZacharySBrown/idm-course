# The variation tree — one source, five variants

One 16-bar loop. Five prescribed mutations. Five sibling variants that share DNA because they branched from the same root.

**Reading the tree:**

- **Root** — your source loop, unchanged.
- **Five branches** — each branch applies exactly one named mutation. No combining.
- **Leaves** — the five usable arrangement sections: drum-swap variant, bass-seed variant, filter-sweep variant, reverse variant, new-layer variant.

Coherence is inherited. Because every variant started identical, the sections sound related even when the mutation is dramatic — the reversed cymbal swell in variant 4 and the held sub in variant 5 both sit over the same core arrangement underneath.

This is the generative property. You do not need to *design* the sections to relate; you design one loop, and the branching does the relating. Spec A §5.4, Finish More Music. <Citation bib="finish_more_music_variation" />

**Arrangement tip, again** — often 3–1–2–5–4 reads better than 1–2–3–4–5. Filter sweep up top sets the entry, drum swap at the A, bass seed at the B, new layer at peak, reverse at outro.
