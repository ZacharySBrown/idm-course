# The parable before the method

Parable — in the summer of 1997, The Flaming Lips and Dave Fridmann decided to make an album that played on four CDs simultaneously. This was not a gimmick. It was an arrangement constraint: each CD had to carry a partial version of the song that only made sense in combination with the other three. Halfway through, they realized you cannot split a normal song across four CDs and threw out months of work. The record that shipped, *Zaireeka*, was the first project ever tracked at Tarbox Road. <Citation bib="zaireeka_wikipedia" /> <Citation bib="fridmann_davefridmann_com" />

The lesson for our five-mutations framework this week: the constraint is the generator. If you are stuck, it is because your constraint is not strong enough.

That is the whole week in one sentence. The rest is receipts and knob positions.
