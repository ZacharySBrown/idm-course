[pause 300ms] The Resample-and-Mutate engine. Spec A, section 5.6.

Mr. Bill calls this a core loop, not a technique. Every 8 to 16 bars of finished loop, you do five steps, in this order, and you do not skip step three.

[pause 400ms]

Step one. Route the drum bus, or the full mix if that is what you want, to a new audio track. In the Audio From drop-down, choose Resampling — or choose the bus directly, by name. Either works.

Step two. Record four to eight bars of audio into that track. Arm the input. Hit record. Let it run.

Step three. *Delete the source.* Delete the source MIDI. Or disable the source group. The printed audio is now the only version.

[pause 300ms]

This is the step that producers resist the hardest. You just spent three days programming that MIDI. The idea of deleting it feels like deleting a photograph.

It is not deleting a photograph. It is framing it. The printed audio is the photograph. The MIDI was the negative. You have the photograph.

Step four. Chop the printed audio into slices. Drop it into a Drum Rack with Slice to Transients, or into a Simpler in Slicing mode. Every slice is now a pad.

Step five. Pitch-shift a few slices. Reverse one. Bit-crush one. Three operations per batch. These slices become your B and C section material — your mutations three, four, and five came from this.

[pause 400ms]

A Live 12 tool that pairs perfectly with this. The Sample Offset envelope. On the printed drum loop, draw a Sample Offset envelope with plus or minus eight sixteenth-notes of range. You are now generating new patterns from the same audio, MPC-style, with no new samples. It is beat repeat built into the clip.

[pause 300ms]

And the workflow rule that makes this sustainable. Every 8 to 16 bars, you hit Cmd+J. Consolidate. The bounce is not a chore. The bounce is the arrangement move. If you wait until the end of the session to consolidate, you will have eleven tracks of overlapping clips and no memory of which was which. Consolidate as you go.

[pause 300ms] Delete the source. Commit. Move.
