# Voice is the consistency, not the uniqueness

Twelve weeks in, you have twelve pieces. Listen to them back-to-back. Some will sound like they were made by twelve different producers. That is the point of the course, and also the problem you now have.

Voice is not the one idea that is unique to one track. Voice is *what recurs across tracks*.

- Your kick-to-the-root move from Week 5, used again in Week 7, then again in Week 10 — that is voice.
- Your tendency to leave 2 bars of silence before the drop, Autechre-style from Week 8 — if you did it twice, that is voice.
- The Fridmann-bass-distortion setting you discovered in Week 4 and reached for every time the low end felt thin — that is voice.

What is *not* voice: the Week 9 vocal-chop experiment you never did again. The Week 2 subtraction exercise that produced a track you cannot listen to. The Week 11 beat-switch that only worked because the stem happened to cooperate.

Voice is what you would do *without thinking*, if you had to make a record in two days. The twelve-week archive tells you what those defaults actually are, not what you told yourself they were. <Citation bib="rick_rubin_creative_act" />

That is the curation exercise.
