# Ship two tracks — Bandcamp private EP, one friend

Two tracks. Not one. Two.

The "one" is a hedge. One track is a single, and a single can be an accident. Two is a *choice* — you picked a pair, you sequenced them, you said these two belong together. That is the smallest unit of a record.

Protocol for each track.

- Open the Live set. Apply the master chain from slides s03–s05. SPAN, EQ, Pro-L 2 or stock Limiter at −1.0 dBTP. <Citation bib="fabfilter_prol2_docs" />
- A/B against two or three references at the same integrated LUFS. Three passes max.
- Bounce **24-bit / 44.1 kHz WAV**, full-mix, no dithering flags needed for 24-bit.
- Name the file per the convention: `yourname_trackname_v01_master.wav`.

Upload both to Bandcamp. Mark the release as "private" — share-link only, not listed on your page. Upload artwork at 3000 × 3000 px. Fill the track titles in lowercase. Genre tag it honestly.

Send the share link to **exactly one friend**. That friend does not need to listen. They do not need to respond. The work has already happened. <Citation bib="jack_conte_ship_it" />

Ship the two files. Close the laptop.
