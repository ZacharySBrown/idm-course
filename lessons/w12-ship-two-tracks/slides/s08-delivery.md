# Delivery — Bandcamp first, distributor later

Three paths. Pick one for this week. You can add the others later.

- **Bandcamp** — direct upload, direct sale, direct download. 10–15% revenue cut. Artist page acts as landing page. Allows "private" releases (unlisted, share-link only) which is exactly the shape of this week's exercise. WAV/FLAC/MP3 all accepted. No ISRC required at upload; you can add it in the track-level metadata. Bandcamp's upload spec is the primary receipt and is not yet in the bibliography; flagged.
- **SoundCloud** — preview-friendly, single-track ecology, weaker paid-download story. Good for a pre-release or a "free track" drop. Poor for an EP.
- **Distributor** — DistroKid, TuneCore, AWAL, CD Baby. They push your track to Spotify, Apple Music, Tidal, every major DSP. Annual fee (DistroKid) or per-release fee (TuneCore). They allocate ISRCs automatically. Use this when you have at least four tracks and you want DSP presence.

For this week's ship exercise: Bandcamp private EP. No DSPs. No distributor fee. The friction is almost zero and the audience is one person. <Citation bib="jack_conte_ship_it" />

Next year, if you have four EP-candidates from the curation exercise, route through DistroKid. For now, Bandcamp.
