Four fields that matter. ISRC, composer credits, genre tags, artwork. One file-naming convention to commit to.

[pause 300ms]

ISRC — International Standard Recording Code. Twelve characters. Allocated per recording, not per track title. If you remix your own track later, that is a new ISRC. If you remaster a track, same ISRC, different version string. [pause 200ms] Distributors allocate ISRCs automatically. Bandcamp does not require one at upload for a private release. If you are moving to DSP distribution later, the distributor will assign them. The RIAA US ISRC agency is the first-party source if you want to allocate your own — flagged in `needs_bib` for a proper entry.

[pause 400ms]

Composer credits. This is where royalty collection lives. If your legal name is one thing and your artist alias is another, pick the one you will register with a performance rights organization — ASCAP, BMI, SOCAN if you are in Canada, PRS if UK, GEMA if Germany — and write it identically on every release. [pause 300ms] Twelve inconsistent composer credits across twelve releases means twelve incomplete royalty payments. The discipline is: one spelling, one pseudonym, on every track, forever.

[pause 400ms]

Genre tags. Bandcamp accepts multiple. Pick two or three tags that are actually accurate. *IDM*, *electronic*, plus one sub-tag — glitch, ambient, breakbeat, whatever describes the record honestly. [pause 200ms] Searchability comes from accuracy. Stuffing the tag list with every genre in the adjacent category does not help.

[pause 400ms]

Artwork. Three thousand by three thousand pixels, JPEG or PNG, under ten megabytes. That is the DSP-common minimum. Lower resolution will upload. It will also look bad on every platform that upscales it.

[pause 500ms]

File naming. [pause 200ms] `artist underscore trackname underscore v zero one underscore mixprint dot wav`. Then `v zero two`, `v zero three` when you re-master. When you export a new master, version it, keep the old one. Never overwrite. Never use emoji in a track title — some distributors silently strip them. Never use exclamation marks. Lowercase is the safer default.
