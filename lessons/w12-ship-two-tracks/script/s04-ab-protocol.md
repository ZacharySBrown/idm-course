You learned the A/B protocol in Week one. It has not changed. The scope has tightened. [pause 300ms]

Pick two or three reference tracks. Same genre as what you are mastering. Not your favorite tracks — a common mistake. Your favorite tracks include a Kate Bush record from 1985 that was mastered to a different standard on a different medium for a different room. [pause 200ms] That is not the reference. The reference is a recent track by a producer whose mastering you would accept next to yours on a playlist. Recent meaning mastered under current streaming-era norms.

[pause 400ms]

Drop the reference tracks on adjacent audio tracks in your Live session. Adjacent to your master bus, solo-able, switchable. Insert a Utility plugin on each reference track. Also insert Utility on your own master. [pause 200ms] Now the level-matching. Loop a thirty-second section of each track in turn. Adjust the Utility gain until Youlean reads the same integrated LUFS on all three sources over that 30-second window. They are now level-matched. *Loudness bias* is neutralized.

[pause 400ms]

Now you listen. Thirty-second chunks, switching between tracks. Five axes, written down on paper, same axes from Week one. [pause 300ms] Low-end energy — is the sub carrying weight, or is the kick body doing the work. Midrange weight — is the track thick in the 200 to 500 hertz band, or thin. Top-end air — does the 10 kilohertz and above sound present, or did your limiter eat it. Stereo width at the chorus — does the mix widen at the climax, or stay narrow. Transient snap on the snare or the kick — is the percussion cutting, or rounded and mushy.

[pause 500ms]

The goal is not to beat the references. The goal is to not embarrass yourself next to them. *Translation*, not *competition*. Write the differences down. Make one adjustment. Re-bounce. Re-check.

[pause 300ms]

Three passes, max. After the third pass, the improvements are smaller than your perceptual threshold and you are wasting time. [pause 200ms] ~Andrew Huang's half-decibel rule applies here.~ Commit the master. Move to the next track.
