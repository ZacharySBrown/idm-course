Two tracks. [pause 300ms] Not one. Two.

The "one" is a hedge. [pause 300ms] One track is a single, and a single can be an accident. You bounced a demo that happened to finish itself. Maybe you meant it. Maybe you got lucky. One track does not prove anything about your intent.

Two is a *choice*. You picked a pair. You decided they belong together. You sequenced them — even if the sequence is just *this one, then this one*. That is the smallest unit of a record. [pause 200ms] Two is what the course has been building toward since Week one, even when the syllabus said twelve.

[pause 500ms]

Protocol, per track. [pause 200ms]

Open the Live set. Apply the master chain we walked through in slides two through five. SPAN on analyzer. Your EQ. Optional master compressor — I did not spend a slide on this because at this stage, less is more, but a two-to-one ratio bus compressor at a half-decibel of gain reduction on loud sections is fine if you already know what a bus compressor does. Pro-L two or stock Limiter at minus one dBTP. Youlean after.

[pause 400ms]

A/B against two or three references at the same integrated LUFS. Three passes, max. [pause 300ms]

Bounce. Twenty-four bit, forty-four point one kilohertz, WAV. No dithering flag needed at twenty-four bit. [pause 200ms] Name the file — `yourname underscore trackname underscore v zero one underscore master dot wav`. Same convention, both tracks.

[pause 500ms]

Open Bandcamp. Create a new release. Mark it *private* — share link only, not listed on your public artist page. Upload both WAVs. Upload artwork at three thousand by three thousand pixels. [pause 200ms] Track titles in lowercase, no emoji, no exclamation marks. Genre tag honestly. Fill the composer field with your consistent name or alias.

[pause 400ms]

Save. Bandcamp generates a private share link. [pause 300ms]

Send that link to exactly one friend. [pause 300ms] One. Not a group text. Not a Discord. One human being whose opinion you trust and whose disappointment you can survive. [pause 200ms] That friend does not need to listen. That friend does not need to respond. The work has already happened. Jack Conte's line about shipping, which I will not quote directly because the cite is link-only in the bibliography, is approximately — *the track is finished when the track is uploaded, not when the track is loved*.

[pause 400ms]

Ship the two files. ~Close the laptop.~
