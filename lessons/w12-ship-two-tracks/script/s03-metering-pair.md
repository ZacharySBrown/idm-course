Two plugins on the master bus. Both free. Both enough. [pause 300ms]

Youlean Loudness Meter 2. Voxengo SPAN.

Youlean does LUFS. Integrated LUFS over the whole track, short-term LUFS in a rolling three-second window, true peak, loudness range, history graph. You insert it *last* on the master bus — after your limiter, after everything. It does not process audio. It only measures. [pause 200ms] Integrated LUFS is the number to watch, and you read it over a full playthrough, not over the first four bars. If you stop the transport at bar eight and the readout says minus fourteen, that is not your integrated LUFS. That is eight bars of *intro*. Quieter intros always read quieter. Play the track end to end.

[pause 400ms]

SPAN is a spectrum analyzer. Frequency on the x-axis, amplitude on the y-axis, long-time averaging optional, left-right and mid-side views separate. You use SPAN to check three things — [pause 200ms] one, sub energy below 60 hertz, which tends to be invisible on headphones and monitors alike. Two, midrange buildup around 300 hertz, which is where tracks get muddy. Three, the air above 10 kilohertz, which is either present, or the track sounds dull, or you hyped it to compensate for a dull monitor and now everyone else's treble is too bright.

[pause 400ms]

Insert order on the master. SPAN first, as an analyzer. Your EQ, compressor, limiter in whatever chain you built. Then Youlean *after* the limiter. Youlean reads the thing that will be exported. SPAN reads the thing you are shaping. They are not interchangeable.
