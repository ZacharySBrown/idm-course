Three delivery paths. Pick one for this week. You can layer the others on later. [pause 300ms]

Bandcamp. SoundCloud. A distributor.

[pause 400ms]

Bandcamp first. Direct upload, direct sale, direct download. The revenue cut is ten to fifteen percent depending on whether the buyer uses an app. Your artist page functions as a landing page — one URL you can send to somebody that contains the record. [pause 200ms] Bandcamp supports private releases, which are unlisted on your public artist page and only accessible via a share link. That posture is exactly the shape of this week's ship exercise — you are not doing a marketing rollout, you are finishing a record and sending it to one friend. Private release. WAV, FLAC, and MP3 are all accepted; WAV is what I recommend for the master. ISRC is not required at upload; you can add it at the track-metadata level if your distributor allocated one. [pause 200ms] The Bandcamp upload spec is not yet in the course bibliography — flagged as needs-bib.

[pause 400ms]

SoundCloud. Preview-friendly, single-track-centric, weaker paid-download story since the 2010s. Fine for a loosie — a free track drop, a single. Poor for an EP because SoundCloud's album affordance has never been good. [pause 300ms] Not this week.

[pause 400ms]

Distributor. DistroKid, TuneCore, AWAL, CD Baby, a dozen others. They push your track to Spotify, Apple Music, Tidal, Amazon, YouTube Music, Deezer — the major DSPs. DistroKid's model is an annual subscription fee, unlimited uploads. TuneCore charges per release. [pause 200ms] Both allocate ISRCs automatically. Both take some kind of cut or fee depending on the tier. These are the path when you have *at least four* finished tracks and you want DSP presence. For two tracks and a ship-the-EP exercise, they are overkill.

[pause 500ms]

This week: Bandcamp private EP. Friction is near zero. Cost is nothing. Audience is one person. [pause 200ms] The point of Bandcamp-first is that you finish, upload, and ship within an hour — not two weeks of waiting on a distributor review queue.

[pause 300ms]

Next year, with four EP candidates from the curation exercise, route through DistroKid. For now — Bandcamp. ~Upload the WAVs and go.~
