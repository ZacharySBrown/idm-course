Open the demo session. It lives at `live-sets/w05-drum-tuning-demo.als`. That's relative to the lesson directory — wherever you've cloned the course materials, the .als is in the week-five folder under `live-sets/`. [pause 200ms]

Here's what's inside, so you're not hunting.

One audio track, muted, carrying `express_dry.wav` from the stemforge render. It's muted because it's a reference, not a stem. Unmute it to A/B against your tuned version. Re-mute before you bounce.

One MIDI track with a Drum Rack. Three pads populated with stemforge one-shots — a kick on C1, a snare on D1, a closed hat on F-sharp one. Each pad runs a Simpler in Classic mode. No other devices on the chain. No compression, no EQ, no saturation. The point of the session is to isolate the tuning move.

Two locators in Arrangement view. Locator one at zero seconds, labeled "dry." Locator two at fifteen seconds, labeled "tuned." This matches the deliverable geometry — fifteen seconds of untouched drums, fifteen seconds of your tuned version.

One written reference marker on the Drum Rack showing the target note. I've set it to E1. You can change it to whatever your track's root is.

The tuning values on the Drum Rack are *deliberately* wrong at session start. Kick is at its Simpler-default transpose of zero. Snare is at zero. Your job is to move them. [pause 200ms] Don't try to guess the right values from these slides — use your ear, against the reference.

Bounce via **Export Audio/Video**, or right-click the drum track and **Freeze** then **Flatten** to commit the tuning into a new audio clip. <Citation bib="ableton_live_12_manual" /> Either works. Freeze-and-flatten is the faster path if you want to iterate.

The session is a scaffold. The solution is your ear. [pause 200ms]
