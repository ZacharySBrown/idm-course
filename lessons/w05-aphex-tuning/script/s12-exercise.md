The exercise. [pause 300ms]

Tune a kick to the root. Tune a snare to the fifth. Thirty seconds. Bounce. Ship.

That's the whole thing. Here's the protocol.

Step one. Open the demo session — `live-sets/w05-drum-tuning-demo.als` — or use any session of your own that has a kick one-shot and a snare one-shot in a Drum Rack. The session doesn't matter. The tuning does.

Step two. Pick a root. E minor is fine. The demo session is in E. If you're working on a track of your own, use that track's key. The exercise works in any key.

Step three. First fifteen seconds of your bounce — drums at source pitch. Do nothing. Just let the loop play, unmodified, so you hear the starting point. [pause 200ms]

Step four. Second fifteen seconds — kick transposed to land at the root. That is roughly minus eighteen to minus twenty-two semitones from a typical kick sample's original pitch. Land the fundamental, not the named note — use your ear against a reference tone. Then snare detuned to the fifth — roughly plus five to plus nine semitones above the root.

Step five. Export a thirty-second stereo WAV. File name `w05_`, your name, `_tuned-ab.wav`. That's the deliverable. <Citation bib="stemforge_repo" />

Then the write-up. One sentence per drum. What *appeared* in the low end when you detuned it — not what you wanted to appear, what you *heard* appear. Two sentences total. ~Yes, really — two sentences.~

The time box. Forty-five minutes maximum. If you are still moving Transpose at minute forty, you have stopped doing the exercise and started designing the kick. Commit. Bounce. Move on. [pause 300ms]

This is the week. Next week we layer four kick elements on top of each other the way Four Tet does. This week you just tune two drums. [pause 400ms]

Go.
