# Express Yourself — dry kick vs tuned kick, on tape

Two renders, both from Charles Wright's "Express Yourself" through the stemforge `idm_crushed` pipeline. One is the kick at source pitch. The other is the same kick transposed until the fundamental lands on E1 — ~41 Hz, the root of the demo track.

Listen for the low end to resolve. Not get louder. *Resolve.*

<Citation bib="stemforge_repo" /> — recipe `w05-express-tuned-ab`, dry and wet renders written to `build/audio/stemforge-renders/w05/`.

The spectrograms tell the story faster than the A/B. The dry render shows a broadband transient — sub-thump, click, body — with no stable fundamental. The tuned render shows the same transient plus a sustained fundamental line at ~41 Hz that didn't exist in the source.

That line is the bassline. It came from the kick. Nothing else in the session changed.

This is the entire lesson, rendered. The rest is how.
