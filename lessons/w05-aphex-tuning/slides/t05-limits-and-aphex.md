# 7-limit, 11-limit — where Aphex lives and 12-TET can't follow

<term key="prime_limit">Prime limit</term> in just intonation = the largest prime number used in any ratio of the scale. 5-limit uses 2, 3, 5 — the Western Renaissance palette. 7-limit adds the blues/barbershop 7:4 and 7:6 intervals. 11-limit adds the quarter-tone-ish 11:8 tritone and 11:9 neutral third.

Why this matters for the lesson's theme:

- **5-limit** — 12-TET approximates it with ≤14 cents of error. Listenable.
- **7-limit** — 12-TET has no good approximation of 7:4. The tempered minor seventh is +31 cents sharp. This is the interval Aphex reaches for when the Syrobonkers interview mentions his Cirklon pattern carrying "1–20 cent per-step micro-pitch offsets." The offsets are pulling the pattern toward 7-limit targets that 12-TET can't name. <Citation bib="aphex_syrobonkers_interview" />
- **11-limit** — no approximation at all in 12-TET. A 11:8 tritone sits at 551 cents, 49 cents off from the piano's tritone. You can only get there with a loaded tuning.

"When it's right in tune, something's slightly off" — when the tuning is 7-limit JI, the tempered piano part next to it sounds wrong, not the JI part. <Citation bib="aphex_pitchfork_microtuning" />
