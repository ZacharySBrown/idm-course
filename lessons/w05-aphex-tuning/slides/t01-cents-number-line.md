# The cents number line — what you're actually counting

Before gear: the unit. **One octave = 1200 cents. One semitone = 100 cents.** Cents are linear where frequency is logarithmic — doubling Hz advances you 1200 cents, not 2×. <Citation bib="ableton_live_12_manual" />

A worked example for the lesson's demo key (E minor):

- **E1** fundamental: 41.20 Hz (the Express Yourself tuned-kick target).
- **B1** the fifth: 61.74 Hz. That's E1 + 700 cents in <term key="twelve_tet">12-TET</term>, or a clean 3:2 ratio (E1 × 1.5 = 61.80 Hz) in <term key="just_intonation">just intonation</term>.
- **G1** the minor third: 49.00 Hz. 12-TET puts it at +300 cents. Pure JI minor third (6:5) puts it at 49.44 Hz — about +16 cents sharp of the tempered note.

The sixteen-cent gap is audible on a sustained pad. It is inaudible on a 50ms kick transient. This is why <term key="microtuning">microtuning</term> kicks is mostly a transpose job, and microtuning pads is where the philosophy fight starts.
