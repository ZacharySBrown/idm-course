# Snare to the fifth — harmony from the backbeat

Kick to root is the headline move. Snare to the fifth is what makes the drum bus sound composed rather than sampled.

Load a snare one-shot in a Drum Rack pad, Simpler, Classic. If the track is in E minor, the fifth is B. Transpose until the snare body lands there — usually +5 to +9 semitones, then 10–20 cents of Detune.

Listen on the 2 and 4. The backbeat stops being impact — it suggests the chord. You now have drums carrying the root (kick) and the fifth (snare). A power chord played by percussion. <Citation bib="aphex_pitchfork_microtuning" />

Extensions, once it works:

- Hats to the octave — high-pass at 3 kHz.
- Tom fills to scale degrees.
- Second kick layer at root −12 for sub.

Then stop. Over-tuning turns drums into a chime.
