# How Aphex programs a microtuning, step by zero

The Korg manual documents the surface. The method is from the Pitchfork piece and the Syrobonkers interview: <Citation bib="aphex_pitchfork_microtuning" /> <Citation bib="aphex_syrobonkers_interview" />

1. **Pick a reference pitch** — not A=440. RDJ's SoundCloud dumps carry patches tuned to A=432 or A=444 depending on the track. The Monologue's master tune (±50 cents) sets the global offset before the scale does anything.
2. **Load or write a scale** — James uses six slots. Scales are not transposed versions of 12-TET; they are idiosyncratic per-track constructions. Typical move: build a scale where the 3rd and 7th degrees are 7-limit JI, the rest is 12-TET, then motion-sequence 1–20 cent offsets on sustained steps.
3. **"Like casting light on a rough surface."** That's his phrase from the Cirklon interview. The microtuning is not a system — it is a texture applied to the same pattern played twice, producing different patterns of beating. <Citation bib="aphex_syrobonkers_interview" />
4. **The patch dictates the scale** — every Monologue preset carries its scale as part of the sound. You don't pick a scale and play patches in it. You recall a patch and the scale arrives pre-bound.

The "right in tune sounds slightly off" one-liner is shorthand for this workflow. The *sound* is the tuning, and the tuning belongs to the patch, and the patch belongs to the song. <Citation bib="aphex_pitchfork_microtuning" />

Three levels of binding. One knob.
