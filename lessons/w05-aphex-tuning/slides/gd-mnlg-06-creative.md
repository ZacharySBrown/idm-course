# What else the Monologue-pattern does — creative uses beyond leads

Microtuning is not only for leads. Four moves past the stereotype:

- **Tuned drum rack** — load the same `.scl` you used for the lead synth into Tuning Systems. Map kick one-shots across a Drum Rack at different MIDI notes. Because Simpler respects Tuning Systems' semitone quantization on melodic mode (not Classic), dropping into Melodic mode + loading a JI scale + placing kicks at scale-degree notes gives you scale-degree-tuned kicks for free.
- **Drone beds in 17-EDO** — a sustained Operator note under a 12-TET drum kit. The 17-EDO neutral third clashes with the kit's tempered harmonics and creates audible beating — a drone that pulses without a <term key="lfo">LFO</term>.
- **Microtuning as modulation** — automate Tuning Systems' load-slot (Live 12.2+) or use MTS-ESP to swap scales mid-phrase. Same melody, three different tunings, back-to-back. The listener hears modulation that has no conventional name.
- **The Novation Bass Station II "AFX Mode" sibling** — same per-key parameter lock philosophy; James advised Novation too. <Citation bib="novation_bass_station_ii_afx" />

Stop treating microtuning as a one-shot effect. It is a *scale* you compose against.
