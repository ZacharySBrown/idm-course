# Just intonation vs equal temperament — the ratio war

<term key="just_intonation">Just intonation</term> tunes every interval to a small-integer frequency ratio. Perfect fifth = 3:2. Major third = 5:4. Minor third = 6:5. Minor seventh = 7:4. Beat-free. Ancient.

<term key="twelve_tet">Equal temperament</term> tunes every interval to a fixed 100-cent step. It is deliberately wrong on every interval except the octave, so that every *key* sounds equally acceptable.

| Interval | Just ratio | Just (cents) | 12-TET (cents) | Error |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Perfect fifth | 3:2 | 701.96 | 700.00 | −1.96¢ |
| Major third | 5:4 | 386.31 | 400.00 | +13.69¢ |
| Minor third | 6:5 | 315.64 | 300.00 | −15.64¢ |
| Minor seventh | 7:4 | 968.83 | 1000.00 | +31.17¢ |

The minor-seventh row is where "right in tune sounds slightly off" stops being a vibe. A 12-TET dominant seventh chord is 31 cents sharp of the pure 7:4 harmonic. Aphex's <term key="seven_limit">7-limit pitches</term> on Syro-era pads are un-reachable from a piano keyboard. <Citation bib="aphex_pitchfork_microtuning" />

Load a `.scl` with those ratios and your Operator plays them. That is the entire Tuning Systems story.
