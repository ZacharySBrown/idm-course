# Yamaha Disklavier — a MIDI-driven acoustic piano

Yamaha's Disklavier line, launched 1987, is an acoustic grand (or upright) with solenoids under every key and pedal. Send it MIDI; the solenoids play the keys; the strings speak. The piano is a real piano with real hammers and real strings — the difference is the motor. <Citation bib="wikipedia_disklavier" />

The Drukqs-era Aphex signature: send a MIDI sequence that no human has fingers for, then **mic the piano body, not the soundboard**. Every key-strike ends with a solenoid click. Those clicks become the record's secondary rhythm track. "Avril 14th," "Nanou 2" — listen past the melody; the clicks are playing a pattern of their own.

From RDJ's SoundCloud metadata: *"played & programmed customised Yamaha Disklavier Pro, Recorded To Nagra IVS 5."* The Nagra tape is the color. The Disklavier is the rhythm section.

This deep-dive: how the solenoid behaves, what "prepared Disklavier" means after John Cage, and how to fake the click-rhythm layer without owning a Disklavier.
