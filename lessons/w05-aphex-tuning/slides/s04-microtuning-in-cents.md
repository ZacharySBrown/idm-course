# What microtuning actually is, in cents

One semitone = 100 cents. One octave = 1200 cents. Cents are linear; frequency is logarithmic.

Three regimes worth naming:

- **±1–7 cents** — animation range. The shimmer you hear when two oscillators are slightly detuned. Aphex uses this on sustained pad notes. <Citation bib="aphex_pitchfork_microtuning" />
- **±15–30 cents** — non-12-TET territory. Just intonation, meantone, Bohlen-Pierce. Load these as Scala files into Live 12. <Citation bib="ableton_live_12_manual" />
- **±100 cents = ±1 semitone** — transposition. The knob you turn for kicks.

The Korg Monologue AFX has microtuning built in — per-key cent offsets, user-programmable scales, six RDJ-designed presets. <Citation bib="korg_monologue_afx_manual" /> You do not need the hardware. Simpler has the same knob.
