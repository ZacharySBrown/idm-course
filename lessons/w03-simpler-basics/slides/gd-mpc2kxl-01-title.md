# Akai MPC2000XL — the Simpler predecessor

A contradiction to open the deep-dive.

The MPC2000XL is not a Drum Rack. It is not even obviously a sampler — it is primarily a 32-track sequencer that happens to ship with 16-voice 16-bit sampling bolted on. Akai introduced it in 2000, a successor to the MPC2000 (1997), and built a machine that defined the Prefuse 73 / Madlib / Dilla-era hip-hop production aesthetic for the next decade.

Why this deep-dive belongs in Week 3: the workflow Ableton calls *Drum Rack + Simpler in Slicing mode + resampling to a new track* is the MPC2000XL workflow translated to software. The pads, the chop mode, the Note Repeat, the resample button — Ableton's modern sampling stack is MPC vocabulary with a DAW under it.

Scott Herren (Prefuse 73) made two albums — *Vocal Studies + Uprock Narratives* (2001) and *One Word Extinguisher* (2003) — on exactly this machine plus a pair of turntables into Pro Tools.
