# Parallel modern workflows — who still works this way

The MPC2000XL workflow survived. It migrated. Three current examples.

- **Madlib on the SP-303 and later Akai MPC Live II**. Same ladder — sample, chop, pitch, resample — on different hardware. His *Shades of Blue* (2003) and the later *Madvillainy* sessions are entirely this method. The takeaway is not *get the same hardware*; it is *sample → chop → resample is the unit of work.*
- **Knxwledge and the MPC Renaissance**. The Renaissance is a hardware controller for MPC software on a computer; the workflow is identical to the 2000XL with 2024 disk space. Knxwledge's 2010s output is MPC-native beats resampled into Ableton for arrangement.
- **The Push 3 standalone**. Ableton shipped a standalone Push in 2023 that runs Live's sampling stack on embedded hardware. It is a Drum Rack with pads. The ladder works — sample, slice to MIDI, resample to audio track, re-slice. Push 3 is the 2026 equivalent of what the MPC2000XL was in 2001: a hardware-first sampler-sequencer with a screen you are allowed to ignore.

The throughline: the method outlived the metal. If you understand the ladder, the box is interchangeable.
