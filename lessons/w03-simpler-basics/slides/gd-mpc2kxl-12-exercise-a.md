# Exercise A — replicate the Prefuse ladder in Ableton

**Deliverable.** One `.wav` file. 8 bars. Project tempo. A single vocal source taken through two ladder cycles.

Steps:

1. Pick one vocal phrase from the stemforge `w03-express-oneshots` output — ideally a 2-4 second chunk with syllabic content (not a sustained note).
2. Drop the phrase onto an audio track. Do **not** warp it. This is the Prefuse constraint — timing drift is the feel.
3. Right-click the clip → **Slice to New MIDI Track**. Slice by Region, 16 slices. Call the new track `Chop 1`.
4. Program 4 bars of MIDI across the 16 pads. Do not re-play the original phrase; rearrange. Use unusual pad orders; repeat fragments; leave gaps.
5. On a new audio track, set the input to `Chop 1`. Record-arm. Play the 4-bar pattern and resample to a fresh audio clip.
6. Drop that resampled clip onto another new audio track. **Slice to New MIDI Track** again — this is ladder rung 2. Call it `Chop 2`.
7. Program another 4 bars on `Chop 2`. Now you are chopping a chop.
8. Bounce all 8 bars from both `Chop 1` and `Chop 2` sequences as the final deliverable. File: `w03_<yourname>_prefuse_ladder.wav`.

Time-box: sixty minutes.
