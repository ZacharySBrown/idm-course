# Interview receipts — Herren on limitation as aesthetic

Receipts first. The ladder is not folk wisdom — it is how Herren explicitly describes his method.

On the MPC as a constraint, from XLR8R:

> *"I value the artistic limits imposed by the more primitive technology. I feel they help me focus on the song, instead of the production."*

On his relationship to the Miami/Schematic IDM aesthetic of the early 2000s:

> *"Trying to fake what they did on computers with the MPC."*

On timing — and this is the one that matters for how you program patterns:

> *"The way I chop samples is kind of careless; I don't try to match bpms and shit."*

Translate: Prefuse 73 did not warp. He chopped at whatever the record happened to be at, finger-drummed over it, let the timing drift be the feel, and resampled. The carelessness is the point. A warped grid-locked recreation of a Prefuse beat will sound sterile no matter how accurate the chops are.

On the source material, from the same interview:

> *"I don't care where they came from, I don't care how rare they are — I'll find a way to make it sound like you haven't heard it before... If you're going to chop your shit somehow, or find a small loop, you're not doing anything."*

The ethic: transformation is the permission.
