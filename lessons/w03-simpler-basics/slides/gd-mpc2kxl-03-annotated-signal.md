# Annotated tour (2/2) — chop mode, resample, track ghost

The second annotated photo highlights the left side of the faceplate — the buttons that make the chopping workflow function.

- **Sample/Record** button (MODE 6) — holds you in sample-record mode while you drop in audio from the rear RCA inputs. 44.1 kHz, 16-bit, mono or stereo.
- **Trim/Edit Sample** (MODE 6 → F4) — the <term key="chop_mode">Chop Shop</term> and 16-Levels pages live here. Chop Shop auto-slices by threshold, regions, or equal divisions; 16-Levels spreads a single sample across 16 pads at chromatic semitones.
- **Resample** — in OS 1.7+, press and hold during playback to record the MPC's stereo output back into a new sample slot. This is the *ladder rung* — chop a pattern, play it, resample it, re-chop the resample.
- **Track Mute** page — each of the 64 tracks can be muted live from the pads. Serves as a performance mixer and as a <term key="track_ghost">track ghost</term> — mute every track except one, solo-audition, confirm a hit.
