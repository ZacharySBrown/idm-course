Simpler versus Sampler. The question that shows up in every Ableton forum, with a thousand confused answers.

[pause 400ms]

The short version — and the Ableton Live 12 Reference Manual says this on the Simpler page in so many words — is: **Simpler has Warp. Sampler does not.**

That is the single fact that decides ninety percent of the choice.

[pause 500ms]

If your sample needs to stay locked to project tempo — if it is a looped break, a time-stretched pad, a sliced groove, anything where the playback speed should follow the host tempo regardless of pitch — use Simpler. Warp is right there.

If your sample needs multi-sample zones — different samples triggered by different velocity ranges, different key ranges, different round-robin slots — use Sampler. That is what Sampler was built for. Sampler has a Zone Editor. Simpler does not.

If you want crossfade between layers — two samples blending continuously as velocity goes from forty to ninety — use Sampler.

If you want key-tracked filter modulation depth — filter cutoff tracked one-for-one to pitch — use Sampler. Simpler has key tracking in the filter, but the amount range is smaller.

[pause 500ms]

Practical rule for this course.

[pause 300ms]

Drum Racks — Simpler on every pad. Or Drum Sampler, which is Simpler-plus-three-tools. You almost never need round-robin on a Drum Rack pad; if you do, you probably want two layered pads with different samples and a probability chain, not a Sampler.

Chromatic vocal chops — Simpler in Classic mode. One chunk across sixteen keys. Warp on for tempo lock. Week 9 builds this from scratch.

Sampler shows up in Week 6 when we layer snares. A three-layer snare with velocity crossfade between soft, medium, and loud captures sounds like a real drummer. That is a Sampler job.

[pause 500ms]

One more thing, because the marketing confuses this — Simpler is not a *simplified* Sampler. They are not two rungs of a ladder. They are different tools that happen to share a waveform display. Ableton ships Simpler in Intro because it is a single-sample instrument with a sensible feature set, not because it is training wheels for Sampler.

[pause 300ms]

If anyone tells you "you should upgrade from Simpler to Sampler once you are ready" — they are selling you something. The question is not which is better. The question is: does your sample need warp, or does it need zones?

Answer that. Pick the tool. Move on.
