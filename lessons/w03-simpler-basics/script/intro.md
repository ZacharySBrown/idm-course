Week three of IDM Production. *Simpler, Drum Rack, one-shots.*

[pause 400ms]

This is the first device-tutorial week of the course. Weeks one and two were about listening and about arrangement — habits of mind, not parameter values. This week we slow down and read the manual for the two devices every drum track you make will pass through. Simpler. Drum Rack.

[pause 300ms]

The content is concrete. Mode tabs. Fade times in milliseconds. Choke groups. Velocity curves. There are specific numbers to write down, and there is a bounce to ship at the end.

We also meet stemforge for the first time in earnest. Week one used it to demonstrate stem separation; this week we use its *forge* pipeline — the one that extracts per-drum-type one-shots from a source track using LarsNet classification. The source track is *Express Yourself*, Charles Wright and the Watts 103rd Street Rhythm Band, 1970 — a track you have almost certainly heard sampled and may not recognize from the title.

[pause 400ms]

Your prerequisites — Week One, *Listen like an engineer, subtract like a producer*, and Week Two, *The Subtraction method, finish by removing*. If you missed Week Two's four-bar arrangement exercise, do it before this one. Habits stack.

[pause 300ms]

Receipts. The Ableton Live 12 Reference Manual is the canonical source for every parameter name in this lesson. When the script says *Simpler has three modes*, that is the Reference Manual, not folk wisdom. The one interview citation you will hear is Kieran Hebden on *Tape Notes* episode 140, who spends most of that episode explaining why he does not use any sampler at all. We hold his position up as a counterexample and then proceed without him.
