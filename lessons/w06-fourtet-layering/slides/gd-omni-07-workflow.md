## Technique — Hebden's alphabetical browse-and-grab

Step by step, reconstructed from `Tape Notes #140` and album sleeve notes for *Three*.

1. Open session. Drop a MIDI clip with a rough sketch — two-bar chord loop or a bass figure.
2. Instantiate Omnisphere on the track. Browser opens by default.
3. Pick category — *Bass* for a bass slot, *Pads* for texture, etc.
4. **Browse alphabetically from the top.** Arrow down through the list. Each preset previews on the sketch.
5. Stop at the first one that "fits." Commit. Close browser.
6. Do *not* open the edit view. Do *not* audition patch B or patch C on the same slot once committed.

*"Plectra Sub," "Swaggering Around"* — two patches named by Hebden `Tape Notes #140`. Both are deep in the alphabet, implying he lets the scroll run a long way before commit. Thirty-second decisions, not thirty-minute ones.
