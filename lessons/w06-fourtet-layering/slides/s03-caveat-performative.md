## The can't-be-bothered framing is performative

- On `Tape Notes #140` Hebden frames preset use as laziness — "I can't be bothered." Taken at face value, reads as anti-craft.
- Partly a pose. The same interview documents months of club playbacks tuning the "Daydream Repeat" kick, 40-track sessions, arrangement maps drawn before a sound is placed.
- Honest read: **preset discipline is a constraint, not apathy**. He rules out one rabbit hole (patch design) so the session budget lands on arrangement and mixing.
- Earlier-era Hebden is not a preset user — *Rounds* (2003) is 200–300 source samples, mangled in AudioMulch + Cool Edit Pro. Flagged in `needs_bib`.
- Adopt the constraint. Do not adopt the pose.
