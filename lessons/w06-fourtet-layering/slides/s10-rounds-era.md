## Rounds-era Hebden — same discipline, different decade

- *Rounds* (2003). Estimated 200–300 source samples per album, 20–30 per track. Zak Handbook synthesis — flagged in `needs_bib`, not a confirmed primary interview.
- Tool chain: **AudioMulch** (modular patching host) + **Cool Edit Pro** (pre-Audition destructive editor). Both long superseded; the logic survives.
- Hebden, *Rounds*-era quote surfaced again on `Tape Notes #140`: "A lot of the sounds you hear on Rounds are kind of misleading. You might hear something that sounds like a bass, but it's actually… a guitar or harp… slowed down a lot and reversed and then manipulated."
- Opposite surface, same artist. 2003 — mangled samples as palette. 2022 — factory presets as palette.
- Through-line: constrain the material, spend the session on arrangement. On "Unspoken" — 2.5-hour train listening to the central loop, then 40 audio tracks filled into a pre-existing map.

Palette is negotiable. Arrangement discipline is not.
