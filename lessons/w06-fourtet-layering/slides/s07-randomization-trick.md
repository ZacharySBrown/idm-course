## Looking At Your Pager — 15% velocity, 15% octave

- `Tape Notes #140`. The KH track "Looking At Your Pager." Used on "pretty much everything" on *Three*.
- Verbatim: "randomization on the velocity of the midi notes… random 15%. And then… 15% of the time to pick one of the notes and play either an octave up or an octave below."
- Result: "little sparkles and twinkles." Pattern never locks. Every playback differs.
- Consequence, his words: "the version on the vinyl will be different probably to the version that's digitally available." Two valid masters. Rendered off a probabilistic source.
- Two parameters. Velocity ±15% of current value. 15% per-note probability of ±1 octave transposition. That is the whole trick.
