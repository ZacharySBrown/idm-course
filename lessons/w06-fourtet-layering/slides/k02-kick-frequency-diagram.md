## Kick-stack frequency bands — one layer per band

Visualizing the stack as a frequency-domain split. Each horizontal bar = one layer's dominant energy range. No EQ cuts — layers are *chosen* for their band.

Approximate energy bands (Zak estimate, not from interview — flagged as inference):

| Layer | Dominant band | Peak frequency | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| Transient click | 3–8 kHz | ~5 kHz | Small-speaker attack |
| Main kick | 80–400 Hz + click 1–3 kHz | ~120 Hz body, ~2 kHz click | Character |
| Sub body | 40–120 Hz | ~60 Hz | Sustained weight |
| Sub sine | 30–60 Hz | ~40 Hz (F1) | PA-only foundation |

The rule: if two layers share a dominant band, one of them is redundant — swap, do not EQ. `Tape Notes #140` — Hebden describes the layers as distinct in function, not as carved-out-of-each-other via EQ.
