## Subliminal layering — the Fridge-era precedent

- `Tape Notes #140`: "little quiet echo-y bits of vocals and noises and little things happening all the time… tons of subliminal little sounds and textures all the time."
- Precedent — his band Fridge tracked live-room mics of players chatting and playing video games between takes, then summed them quietly under the mix with delay. Conversations on the record. Not consciously audible.
- Function, his words: "a way for people to connect to it without them really even understanding why they're connecting to it."
- **Mechanism**: a layer 15–25 dB below the leads, often band-limited, often delayed. Does not compete. Fattens spatial impression.
- **Mix-bus implication**: this is why the record works on laptop speakers. Subliminal layers are additive depth, not rivals for headroom.
