One caveat before we go further. The "I can't be bothered" framing Hebden uses on `Tape Notes #140` has been taken at face value by, roughly, the entire producer internet. That is a mistake.

[pause 400ms]

On the same episode, two things are documented that contradict the laziness pose. One — the "Daydream Repeat" kick was tuned across *months* of club shows, where he would notice that the low end didn't land on a big rig, go home, add another layer, and bring it back out. That is not laziness. That is iterative PA calibration over a touring calendar.

[pause 400ms]

Two — the arrangement of a typical *Three* track is forty audio tracks filled into a pre-existing structural map. He writes the arrangement *first*, before filling the tracks. He has quoted, on an earlier *Rounds*-era interview about "Unspoken," that he rode a two-and-a-half-hour train listening to the central loop before placing any other element. That is the opposite of can't-be-bothered. That is religious attention, just directed away from synth patches.

[pause 400ms]

So when you hear Hebden say he uses presets because he is lazy, translate it. What he means is: *I have ruled out a category of rabbit hole so I can afford to be religious about the categories that matter*. The categories that matter, for him, are arrangement and mixing.

[pause 500ms]

The second half of this caveat. Earlier-era Hebden — *Rounds*, 2003 — is emphatically *not* a preset producer. Estimated 200 to 300 source samples across the album. Built in AudioMulch and Cool Edit Pro, both long superseded. Samples mangled to the point that a bass line is actually a slowed-down harp.

[pause 300ms]

I want to flag that for `needs_bib` — the *Rounds*-era workflow is synthesized in the Zak Handbook from scattered interviews, but we do not have a single primary source pinned down yet. If you cite it in your own notes, call it synthesis, not gospel.

[pause 400ms]

The point: adopt Hebden's constraint. Do not adopt Hebden's pose. The constraint is the useful part.
