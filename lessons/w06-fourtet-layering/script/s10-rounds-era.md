Quick historical pivot before the exercise. It would be easy to walk away from this lesson thinking Four Tet is the preset producer. He is not, and was not. 2003 Hebden is the opposite animal.

[pause 400ms]

*Rounds*, released May 2003 on Domino. The best synthesis we have — flagged as synthesis, not primary citation — puts the per-album sample count at two hundred to three hundred, with twenty to thirty sources per track. I am flagging this in the `needs_bib` array of the lesson yaml. If anyone on the team finds the primary interview that pins the exact number, we update.

[pause 500ms]

The tool chain for *Rounds* was **AudioMulch** — a now-mostly-forgotten modular patching host for live audio routing, popular with IDM producers in the early 2000s — and **Cool Edit Pro**, which Adobe bought and rebranded as Audition. Neither tool still exists in the form Hebden used them. The logic survives.

[pause 400ms]

The logic was — record or sample a source, chop it in Cool Edit to the point that the original is unrecognizable, route it through AudioMulch patches that reshuffle and transform in real time, print the result, and load it into the arrangement as audio. His quote, surfaced again in `Tape Notes #140` — *"a lot of the sounds you hear on Rounds are kind of misleading. You might hear something that sounds like a bass, but it's actually the sound of a guitar or harp or something that's being slowed down a lot and reversed and then manipulated."*

[pause 500ms]

Same artist, twenty years apart, diametrically opposite surfaces. 2003 — manipulated samples as the entire palette. 2022 — factory presets as the entire palette.

[pause 300ms]

What survives both eras is the arrangement discipline. On "Unspoken," from *Rounds* — he has said he rode a two-and-a-half-hour train listening to the central loop before placing any other element. Then he filled forty tracks into a pre-built structural map. On *Three*, twenty years later, the arrangement is *still* the religious part of the session, while the palette is a preset browser.

[pause 400ms]

The through-line — whatever the palette, the session runs on arrangement. The palette is negotiable. The discipline is not.
