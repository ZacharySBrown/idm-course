Exercise. Thirty to sixty minutes. One deliverable.

[pause 400ms]

Build an eight-bar loop at 120 BPM, sitting over `moanin_glitch.wav` from the week six Stemforge render. The loop has a four-layer kick stack on its own group. You render a stereo master of the loop, and you render four individual stems — one per kick layer. You write a plain text notes file that names each layer's one job in one sentence.

[pause 400ms]

Steps.

[pause 200ms]

One — drop `moanin_glitch.wav` on a new audio track. Set warp to Complex Pro. Project tempo, 120 BPM. The Moanin' stem goes on a muted or return channel for the final bounce. It is a reference, not the mix.

[pause 400ms]

Two — create a MIDI group called KickStack. Four MIDI tracks inside. All four route to the group output.

[pause 300ms]

Three — layer one is the main kick. A kick one-shot you already like. Simpler in Classic mode. Do not touch Simpler's front panel beyond loading the sample.

[pause 400ms]

Four — layer two is the sub sine. Operator, a single sine operator, ratio 0.5, short release around 120 milliseconds. Tune to the F of the Moanin' walk by ear. Fundamental around F-one.

[pause 400ms]

Five — layer three is the transient click. Either a noise burst in Operator with a band-pass filter, three to six kilohertz, or the attack transient of a hat sliced in Simpler. Ten-millisecond envelope. Unpitched.

[pause 300ms]

Six — layer four is the sub body. Second Operator sine, one octave below layer two, so F-zero. Release around three hundred milliseconds. Low-pass at 120 hertz on the track.

[pause 400ms]

Seven — solo each layer. Confirm out loud what its one job is. If you cannot say it in a sentence, delete it and start the layer over.

[pause 400ms]

Eight — sum. Render stereo master plus four stems. Write the notes file.

[pause 500ms]

Constraint. No EQ on the individual layers beyond the band-pass on layer three. `Tape Notes #140` — the layers are chosen to not fight. They are not carved to not fight.

[pause 400ms]

Feel the weight. That is the exercise.
